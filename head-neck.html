頭頸部腫瘤

頭頸部解剖、淋巴結分區、口腔癌、口咽癌、喉癌與鼻咽癌：workup、分期、放療劑量、同步化療、保器官治療與關鍵臨床試驗。

Head and neck anatomy, nodal levels, oral cavity cancer, oropharyngeal cancer, laryngeal cancer, and nasopharyngeal cancer: workup, staging, RT dose, concurrent chemotherapy, organ preservation, and key clinical trials.

頭頸部放療的淋巴結選擇原則是：level II–IV 幾乎常規涵蓋；所有 pharynx 類病灶要涵蓋咽後淋巴結；鼻咽癌要涵蓋 level V；聲門下侵犯要涵蓋 level VI；下唇、前口腔舌、前口底要涵蓋 level Ia。若某個 nodal level 已受侵犯，鄰近 level 通常也需進入低劑量 elective volume，例如 level II 陽性時常把 level Ib 與 V 納入低劑量區。

Head and neck tumors can be organized anatomically into the nasal cavity and paranasal sinuses, oral cavity, nasopharynx, oropharynx, larynx, and hypopharynx. These sites have distinct mucosal subsites, nodal drainage patterns, and radiotherapy target-volume implications.

A practical elective-nodal principle is that levels II–IV are commonly covered for many head and neck sites; all pharyngeal sites require retropharyngeal coverage; nasopharyngeal cancer requires level V coverage; subglottic extension requires level VI coverage; and lower lip, anterior oral tongue, and anterior floor-of-mouth tumors require consideration of level Ia. If one nodal level is involved, adjacent nodal levels are often included in the low-dose elective volume.

共通原則

頭頸部放療共通原則：治療前評估、劑量、化療、分割與 OAR

頭頸部放療前需完整評估病人功能狀態、營養、吞嚥、牙齒狀況與腫瘤範圍。標準 workup 包括病史與理學檢查、顱神經檢查、頸部淋巴結檢查、內視鏡檢查、CT neck with contrast、胸部影像，必要時 PET/CT 或 MRI。若預計使用 cisplatin，需確認腎功能、聽力與整體耐受度。放療前牙科評估非常重要，若需要拔牙應盡量在放療前完成，以降低 osteoradionecrosis 風險；放療期間與之後需使用 fluoride。

吞嚥與營養評估也很重要。若病人治療前已無法進食或飲水，可 upfront 放置 feeding tube；若放療中體重下降超過 10%，也常需 feeding tube。語言吞嚥治療師、營養師、牙科、聽力檢查與戒菸諮詢，都是頭頸部放療前的核心流程。

IMRT 是頭頸部放療標準，主要優點是 parotid sparing 以降低 xerostomia，也可降低 mandible、cochlea、brainstem、pharyngeal constrictor 等劑量。PARSPORT 顯示 parotid-sparing IMRT 可降低 grade 2+ xerostomia。

Before head and neck radiotherapy, patients require comprehensive assessment of performance status, nutrition, swallowing function, dental status, and tumor extent. Standard workup includes history and physical examination, cranial nerve examination, cervical nodal examination, endoscopy, contrast-enhanced neck CT, and chest imaging, with PET/CT or MRI when indicated. If cisplatin is planned, renal function, hearing, and overall tolerance must be assessed. Dental evaluation before RT is critical; extractions, if required, should ideally be completed before RT to reduce osteoradionecrosis risk, and fluoride should be used during and after RT.

Swallowing and nutritional evaluation are also essential. If patients cannot eat or drink before treatment, upfront feeding-tube placement can be considered; if weight loss exceeds 10% during RT, a feeding tube is often needed. Speech and swallowing therapy, nutrition, dentistry, audiology, and smoking cessation are core pretreatment components.

口腔癌

口腔癌：subsite、workup、淋巴引流、分期、治療與證據

口腔癌約占頭頸部惡性腫瘤 30%，約 95% 是鱗狀上皮癌。男女比例約 3:2。美國最常見 subsite 是口腔舌與唇。七個主要 subsite 包括：唇、口腔舌前 2/3、口底、齒齦/齒槽嵴、頰黏膜、臼齒後三角、硬顎。主要危險因子是吸菸、酒精、嚼菸、口腔衛生不良、牙周病、不合適假牙造成慢性刺激、檳榔、慢性日曬，尤其唇癌、免疫抑制，以及 Fanconi anemia 或 dyskeratosis congenita 等遺傳症候群。

口腔癌常表現為疼痛、不癒合潰瘍、出血、吞嚥困難、假牙不合、口臭。晚期可出現臉部麻木、舌頭伸出困難或張口困難。理學檢查常見可見或可觸及腫塊/潰瘍，以及頸部淋巴結腫大。檢查時應視診腫瘤、記錄大小與位置、觸診腫瘤邊界、做顱神經與頸部淋巴結檢查，並用 flexible nasolaryngoscopy 排除第二原發癌。影像以 CT neck with contrast、CT chest without contrast 為主；PET/CT 可評估 nodal/distant staging，但評估原發腫瘤有時較困難；若懷疑神經周圍侵犯，考慮 MRI。

Oral cavity cancer accounts for about 30% of head and neck malignancies, and about 95% are squamous cell carcinomas. The male-to-female ratio is about 3:2. In the United States, the most common subsites are oral tongue and lip. The seven major subsites are lip, anterior two-thirds of oral tongue, floor of mouth, gingiva/alveolar ridge, buccal mucosa, retromolar trigone, and hard palate. Major risk factors include smoking, alcohol, chewing tobacco, poor oral hygiene, periodontal disease, chronic irritation from poorly fitting dentures, betel nut, chronic sun exposure especially for lip cancer, immunosuppression, and genetic syndromes such as Fanconi anemia or dyskeratosis congenita.

Oral cavity cancer commonly presents with pain, non-healing ulcer, bleeding, dysphagia, poorly fitting dentures, or halitosis. Advanced disease can cause facial numbness, difficulty protruding the tongue, or trismus. Physical examination may show a visible or palpable mass/ulcer and cervical lymphadenopathy. Examination should include tumor inspection, documentation of size and location, palpation of tumor borders, cranial nerve and neck examination, and flexible nasolaryngoscopy to exclude a second primary. Imaging usually includes contrast-enhanced neck CT and non-contrast chest CT; PET/CT can assess nodal/distant staging but may be less reliable for the primary tumor; MRI is considered when perineural invasion is suspected.

Nodal management is critical in oral cavity cancer. Even in cN0 disease, many patients require elective neck dissection. In general, at least levels I–III are treated; for oral tongue cancers, levels I–IV are often treated; central tumors usually require bilateral neck management; well-lateralized tumors may be treated unilaterally.

口咽癌

口咽癌：HPV/p16 分型、workup、分期、治療與 de-escalation 證據

口咽癌是 HNSCC 最常見部位之一，略超過頭頸部惡性腫瘤 30%；>90% 是鱗狀上皮癌，男女比例約 4:1。四個 subsite 是舌根、顎扁桃、軟顎、後咽壁。口咽癌可分成 HPV/p16-positive 與 HPV-negative 兩類，AJCC 8th edition 將兩者視為不同疾病，因為 HPV-positive 預後明顯較好。

  • HPV-positive：病人常較年輕、非吸菸或輕度吸菸、較常發生於 tonsil/base of tongue，病理常為 nonkeratinizing、basaloid、poorly differentiated，且 p16 upregulated。
  • HPV-negative：較常與重度吸菸飲酒相關，病理常 keratinizing，常見 p53 mutation 與 EGFR amplification。
  • HPV 致癌機轉：E7 會抑制 pRB，造成細胞週期失控並使 p16 過度表現；E6 會促使 p53 degradation。
  • p16 IHC：CAP 建議 p16 IHC 若有至少 70% nuclear and cytoplasmic expression，且強度中到強，可作為 HPV-positive surrogate；若 p16 弱或 <70%，需 confirmatory high-risk HPV testing。

最常見表現是無痛性頸部腫塊，尤其 HPV-positive 病人可因 cystic level II node 表現。其他症狀包括吞嚥困難、吞嚥痛、耳痛。耳痛可由 CN IX 經 Jacobson nerve referred pain。若有口腔舌固定，提示深部肌肉侵犯；張口困難提示 medial pterygoid invasion。

Workup 包括病史與理學檢查、營養評估、吸菸史、BOT palpation、牙科檢查、神經學檢查、flexible laryngoscopy。影像以 CT neck with contrast 評估原發灶，PET/CT 評估分期與 regional lymphadenopathy；若懷疑神經周圍侵犯或顱底侵犯，考慮 MRI。病理可先做 suspicious lymph node FNA，FNA 樣本通常足夠做 HPV testing；確認診斷常需麻醉下詳細檢查、tonsillectomy 或 BOT biopsy。

Oropharyngeal cancer is one of the most common HNSCC sites, accounting for slightly more than 30% of head and neck malignancies; more than 90% are squamous cell carcinomas, with a male-to-female ratio of about 4:1. The four subsites are base of tongue, palatine tonsil, soft palate, and posterior pharyngeal wall. Oropharyngeal cancer is divided into HPV/p16-positive and HPV-negative disease, and AJCC 8th edition treats them as distinct diseases because HPV-positive disease has a substantially better prognosis.

  • HPV-positive: patients are often younger, non-smokers or light smokers, and tumors more often arise in the tonsil or base of tongue; pathology is often nonkeratinizing, basaloid, poorly differentiated, and p16 upregulated.
  • HPV-negative: more strongly associated with heavy tobacco and alcohol exposure; tumors are often keratinizing and commonly show p53 mutation and EGFR amplification.
  • HPV oncogenesis: E7 inhibits pRB, causing cell-cycle dysregulation and p16 overexpression; E6 promotes p53 degradation.
  • p16 IHC: CAP recommends that at least 70% nuclear and cytoplasmic expression with moderate-to-strong intensity can be used as a surrogate for HPV positivity; weak or <70% staining requires confirmatory high-risk HPV testing.

The most common presentation is a painless neck mass, especially in HPV-positive patients with a cystic level II node. Other symptoms include dysphagia, odynophagia, and otalgia. Otalgia can arise from CN IX via Jacobson nerve referred pain. Fixed oral tongue suggests deep muscle invasion; trismus suggests medial pterygoid invasion.

Workup includes history and physical examination, nutritional assessment, smoking history, base-of-tongue palpation, dental examination, neurologic examination, and flexible laryngoscopy. Contrast-enhanced neck CT evaluates the primary tumor, while PET/CT evaluates staging and regional lymphadenopathy; MRI is considered when perineural or skull-base invasion is suspected. Pathology can begin with suspicious lymph-node FNA, which is usually sufficient for HPV testing; definitive diagnosis often requires examination under anesthesia, tonsillectomy, or base-of-tongue biopsy.

Laryngeal cancer is more common in men than women. About two-thirds arise in the glottis, one-third in the supraglottis, and 1–2% in the subglottis. The glottis has sparse lymphatic drainage, so early glottic cancer has low nodal risk; the supraglottis and subglottis have richer lymphatic drainage. The larynx lies approximately at C3–C6, from the hyoepiglottic ligament to the cricoid cartilage. The preepiglottic and paraglottic spaces communicate anterosuperiorly, and invasion of these spaces affects T staging.

Except for the cricothyroid muscle, most laryngeal muscles are innervated by the recurrent laryngeal nerve; injury can cause a fixed midline cord and hoarseness. The cricothyroid is innervated by the superior laryngeal nerve; injury causes mobile but bowed cords, lower-pitched voice, and difficulty reaching high pitch.

The classic presentation of early glottic cancer is hoarseness. Other symptoms include dysphagia, foreign-body sensation, cough, hemoptysis, stridor, and otalgia. Otalgia can arise from CN X branches such as the superior laryngeal nerve or Arnold nerve referred pain.

Workup includes cranial nerve and neck examination, flexible nasolaryngoscopy to assess the primary tumor and vocal cord mobility, and videostroboscopy to assess the vocal cord mucosal wave. Imaging includes contrast-enhanced neck CT and non-contrast chest CT; PET/CT assesses nodal and distant staging; MRI is used if perineural spread is suspected. Pathology often requires examination under anesthesia with triple endoscopy and biopsy: laryngoscopy, bronchoscopy, and esophagoscopy. Pretreatment dental, swallowing, nutrition, hearing, and smoking cessation assessment are also required.

鼻咽癌：EBV、workup、分期、治療架構與關鍵證據

鼻咽癌在美國罕見，約 0.5–2/100,000，但在華南、東南亞、北非等地區高盛行，可達約 25/100,000。鼻咽癌最常與 EBV 感染相關，且通常不以手術為主，因為位置深、接近顱底、顱神經與大血管；手術多保留作 salvage。最常見起源位置是 Rosenmüller 窩。鼻咽解剖邊界：前方是後鼻孔與鼻中隔後緣，後方是 C1/C2，上方是顱底，下方是軟顎，側方是咽鼓管、torus tubarius、Rosenmüller 窩。

  • 危險因子：EBV、鹹魚或保存食物、低蔬果飲食、菸草、家族史與 HPV。
  • EBV：在 endemic area 約與 95% 病例相關，在非 endemic area 也可見於 >20% 病例。
  • 臨床表現：無痛性頸部腫塊、鼻塞或耳悶、頭痛、複視、臉部麻木、吞嚥困難、聲音沙啞、Horner syndrome、CN XI deficit。
  • CN VI：局部顱底侵犯時常最早被壓迫。
  • Jacod’s triad：cavernous sinus invasion 可造成視力喪失、眼肌麻痺、三叉神經痛。
  • Nodal disease：診斷時淋巴結侵犯非常常見，約 75–90%，其中約 50% 為雙側。

  • MAC-NPC meta-analysis：納入 7080 位、26 個 trials，顯示加入 chemotherapy 有約 5 年 6%、10 年 8% 絕對 survival benefit，最大獲益來自 concurrent chemotherapy；在 concurrent chemoRT 基礎上加 induction 或 adjuvant chemotherapy 也可進一步改善 tumor control 與 survival。
  • Stage II NPC 中國 RCT：concurrent cisplatin + RT 比 RT alone 改善 5 年 OS 與 distant metastasis-free survival，但不明顯改善 locoregional recurrence；急性 grade 3–4 毒性較高，晚期毒性相近。
  • Sun et al.：induction TPF → chemoRT 改善 3 年 failure-free survival。
  • Zhang et al.：induction gemcitabine/cisplatin → chemoRT 改善 5 年 OS、FFS、DMFS、LRFS；EBV DNA >4000 copies/mL 的病人獲益最大。
  • DIPPER trial：very locally advanced endemic NPC 在 induction chemotherapy + concurrent chemoRT 後，加 adjuvant camrelizumab 可改善 3 年 EFS；是否適用於非 endemic 或 keratinizing histology 仍需小心。
  • De novo metastatic NPC：中國 phase III trial 顯示，對初始 PF chemotherapy 有 CR/PR 的病人，加 locoregional IMRT 70 Gy 可改善 2 年 OS 與 PFS。

Nasopharyngeal carcinoma is rare in the United States, about 0.5–2 per 100,000, but highly prevalent in southern China, Southeast Asia, and North Africa, reaching about 25 per 100,000. It is strongly associated with EBV and is usually not managed surgically because of its deep location near the skull base, cranial nerves, and great vessels; surgery is generally reserved for salvage. The most common site of origin is the fossa of Rosenmüller. Nasopharyngeal boundaries include the posterior choanae and posterior nasal septum anteriorly, C1/C2 posteriorly, skull base superiorly, soft palate inferiorly, and the eustachian tube, torus tubarius, and fossa of Rosenmüller laterally.

  • Risk factors: EBV, salted fish or preserved foods, low fruit/vegetable intake, tobacco, family history, and HPV.
  • EBV: associated with about 95% of endemic cases and more than 20% of non-endemic cases.
  • Clinical presentation: painless neck mass, nasal obstruction or ear fullness, headache, diplopia, facial numbness, dysphagia, hoarseness, Horner syndrome, and CN XI deficit.
  • CN VI: often the first cranial nerve compressed with skull-base invasion.
  • Jacod’s triad: cavernous sinus invasion can cause visual loss, ophthalmoplegia, and trigeminal neuralgia.
  • Nodal disease: nodal involvement at diagnosis is very common, about 75–90%, and about 50% are bilateral.
  • Distant disease: about 5–11% have metastases at diagnosis; the most common distant sites are bone, lung, and liver.

Workup includes a complete neurologic examination and nasopharyngoscopy. Laboratory testing includes CBC, CMP, and EBV DNA PCR; pretreatment EBV DNA level is prognostic. Imaging includes contrast-enhanced skull base and neck CT and skull-base MRI; MRI is superior for primary tumor extent and neural invasion, while PET/CT is important for distant staging in T3–T4 or N+ patients. Pathology can be obtained from primary biopsy or neck FNA; EBV testing can use LMP IHC or EBER in situ hybridization. Detectable EBV DNA before or after treatment predicts worse PFS/OS, and persistent post-treatment EBV DNA suggests persistent microscopic disease or distant-failure risk.

  • De novo metastatic NPC: a Chinese phase III trial showed that patients with CR/PR to initial PF chemotherapy had improved 2-year OS and PFS when locoregional IMRT to 70 Gy was added.

High-yield take-home messages

  • General head and neck RT: 70/63/56 Gy SIB is used for definitive treatment, 60/57/54 Gy SIB for postoperative treatment, and 66 Gy plus concurrent cisplatin for positive margin or ENE/ECE. Treatment time must not be prolonged. IMRT is standard, and dental, swallowing, nutrition, hearing, and smoking-cessation assessments should not be omitted.
  • Oral cavity cancer: surgery-first is the core principle. Even early disease cannot ignore the neck because occult nodal disease is common; Tata Memorial supports elective neck dissection, and Senti-MERORL supports SLNB in selected T1–T2 cN0 patients. DOI or thickness ≥4 mm is a major warning sign for neck failure.
  • Oropharyngeal cancer: HPV/p16 status is central. HPV-positive disease has good prognosis, but most definitive de-escalation attempts have failed; cetuximab should not routinely replace cisplatin, and HN005 does not support reducing definitive RT dose or replacing cisplatin with nivolumab.
  • Laryngeal cancer: the key is functional preservation and functional assessment. Early glottic cancer responds well to hypofractionated RT with good voice outcomes; if a locally advanced larynx remains functional, concurrent chemoradiotherapy is a larynx-preservation strategy; if it is nonfunctional, total laryngectomy is more appropriate.
  • Nasopharyngeal carcinoma: non-surgical, EBV-driven, and treated by integrated chemoradiotherapy. Stage I can receive RT alone; stage II and above often require concurrent chemoradiotherapy; locally advanced nonkeratinizing, EBV-high-risk patients can be considered for induction gemcitabine/cisplatin or TPF; metastatic patients with good chemotherapy response can receive locoregional RT.

Methods: ➜ 66 Gy (6 MeV wedge pair) vs. ➜ CO2 laser (TLS) 1⁰ endpoint: 2-yr voice quality blinded experts rated voice quality on GRBAS scale, patient self-assessed voice quality and ADL impact, and blinded videostrobe
