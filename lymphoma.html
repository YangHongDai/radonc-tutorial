淋巴瘤

霍奇金與非霍奇金淋巴瘤的放射治療原則。

Radiation for Hodgkin and non-Hodgkin lymphoma.

霍奇金

霍奇金淋巴瘤 (HL)

Early stage favorable: ABVD ×2 + ISRT 20 Gy (GHSG HD10)。

Early stage unfavorable: ABVD ×4 + ISRT 30 Gy (HD11)。

Advanced stage: ABVD ×6 或 escalated BEACOPP；PET-adapted 已成標準 (RATHL、GHSG HD16/17)。

ISRT / INRT (ILROG): 從擴大範圍 EFRT → involved-field → involved-site，降低長期毒性 (二次癌、心臟事件)。

Early stage favorable: ABVD ×2 + ISRT 20 Gy (GHSG HD10).

Early stage unfavorable: ABVD ×4 + ISRT 30 Gy (HD11).

Advanced stage: ABVD ×6 or escalated BEACOPP; PET-adapted approach is standard (RATHL, GHSG HD16/17).

ISRT / INRT (ILROG): Volumes reduced from EFRT → involved-field → involved-site, reducing late toxicity (SMN, cardiac events).

非霍奇金

非霍奇金淋巴瘤 (NHL)

DLBCL: R-CHOP ×6 ± ISRT 30 Gy (partial response 或 bulky)；FLYER — 年輕 low-risk 4 週期 R-CHOP 就夠。

Follicular: Stage I/II — ISRT 24 Gy (Lowry, Hoskin)。晚期無症狀觀察。

MALT lymphoma (胃): H. pylori 根除；持續病灶 24 Gy ISRT。

DLBCL: R-CHOP ×6 ± ISRT 30 Gy (partial response or bulky); FLYER — young low-risk: 4 cycles R-CHOP is sufficient.

Follicular: Stage I/II — ISRT 24 Gy (Lowry, Hoskin). Advanced asymptomatic — watch and wait.

Gastric MALT: H. pylori eradication; persistent disease — 24 Gy ISRT.

EORTC LYSA GELA H10 F/U

Hodgkin, favorable unfavorable · Fav/Unfav: RT in PET negative disease

Raemaekers et al, JCO, 2014 André et al, JCO, 2017 Federico et al, JCO, 2023 · N=1137

Population: Stage I-II HL, above diaphragm

Methods: ABVD x2 → PET → ➜🏆 Standard, non-PET directed, both RER and SER: → ABVD (x1 for F, x2 for U) + 30-36 Gy INRT vs. ➜ PET2 directed INRT: → PET2 negative → additional ABVD (total x6 for U, total x4 for F) → SER PET2 positive → BEACOPP x2 + 30-36 Gy INRT Primary endpoint: noninferiority of PFS, delta of 10%. Superiority stats also included Positive PET was defined as Deauville 3-5

Results: -In favorable 86% achieved negative PET2. -In unfavorable 75% had negative PET2 -PET negative arm stopped early at 1-yr analysis 📌 Fav PET negatives 3ABVD + INRT vs. 4ABVD: 5-yr PFS 99% vs. 87% ⬆️ 10-yr PFS 99% vs. 85%, superior 10-yr OS 100% vs. 98%, not different 10-yr 2nd cancer 4.0% vs. 3.8% 📌 Unfav PET negatives 4ADVD + INRT vs. 6ABVD: 5-yr PFS 92% vs. 90% 5-yr OS 100% in both arms ✅ 10-yr PFS 91% vs. 86%, not noninferior 10-yr OS 95%, not different 10-yr 2nd cancer 6.8% vs. 6.5% -With ABVD alone, 73% failures were soley at original sites No difference in 10-yr adverse effects or 2nd canc…

Interpretation: Observation is not noninferior to INRT in early stage HL, both favorable and unfavorable, with negative PET2. The PET negative arms were closed early due to futility. PFS with INRT was superior in favorable PET negative. Additional cycles of chemotherapy do not replace INRT in this setting. There is no difference in 10-yr adverse effects or second cancer. There is no OS benefit to INRT in CR, but INRT spares some from salvage therapy. In SER PET2 positive, BEACOPPesc improves PFS over ABVD. Chemo and INRT shows good results and allows some to be spared transplant.

UK NCRI RAPID

Hodgkin, favorable unfavorable · Fav/Unfav: RT in PET negative disease

Radford et al, NEJM, 2015 Barrington et al, Radiother Oncol, 2019 · N=602

Population: Stage IA-IIA (about 1/3 unfavorable) Bulky disease excluded

Methods: ABVD x3 → PET → PET3 negative: ➜🏆 30 Gy IFRT vs. ➜obs PET3 positive: ➜🏆 ABVD x1 + 30 Gy IFRT Primary endpoint: noninferiority of PFS, delta of 7% in CI Positive PET was defined as Deauville 3-5

Results: PET3 negative in 75% 📌 PET3 negative (IFRT vs. obs): 3-yr PFS favors RT, 95% vs. 91% (CI exceeded noninferiority boundary of 7) 3-yr OS 97% vs. 99% (p=-.21) Per protocol analysis (12% in RT arm "declined" RT) 3-yr PFS 97% vs. 90% 📌 PET3 positive PFS 88% Only 5.5% of PET positive had transplant PET score of 5 was the best predictor of prognosis. PET score of 1-4, EORTC or GHSH favorable vs. unfavorable not predicitive

Interpretation: Observation is not noninferior to IFRT in early stage HL with negative PET after chemo, and PFS favored IFRT. Favorable or unfavorable was not predictive. There were protocol violations in the RT arm. When analyzed per protocol, RT has superior PFS. In those PET positive, ABVDx4 plus IFRT allowed 88% to avoid transplant or death.

HD16 GHSG

Hodgkin, favorable · Fav: RT in PET negative disease

Fuchs et al, JCO, 2019 Baues et al, IJROBP, 2021 Eichenauer et al, Blood, 2023 · N=1150

Population: Stage I-II HL, favorable NLPHL allowed

Methods: ABVD x2 → PET → ➜ Standard, non-PET directed: 🏆 20 Gy IFRT vs. PET2 directed IFRT: ➜ SER PET2 positive:🏆 20 Gy IFRT ➜ PET2 negative: no RT Primary endpoint: noninferiority of PFS Positive PET was defined as Deauville 3-5

Results: PET2 negative in 66% 📌 PET 2negatives (standard vs. directed): 5-yr PFS 93% IFRT vs. 86% no RT -Infield recurrence 2% vs. 11% -No difference in outfield recurrence, 4-7% both arms 5-yr OS 98% both arms 📌 PET2 positive /PR/ SER using Deauville 4 ADVD+IFRT 5-yr PFS in PET negative 93% ADVD+IFRT 5-yr PFS in PET positive 80% 📌 HD16 + HD17 NLPHL: PET2 negative NLPHL, no RT compared to RT 5-yr PFS 83% without RT, 100% with RT 5-yr OS 100% in both

Interpretation: Observation is not noninferior to IFRT in favorable HL with negative PET2 after chemo. PFS favors RT. In SER PET positive, ABVDx2 plus IFRT allowed 80% to avoid relapse or death. RT is recommended for NLPHL in those PET negative after initial chemotherapy.

EORTC GELA H9F

Hodgkin, favorable · Fav: RT dose, omission of RT

Thomas et al, IJROBP, 2017 · N=783

Population: favorable I-IIB HL

Methods: After 6 cycles EBVP and CRu→ ➜ 🏆 36 Gy IFRT vs. ➜ 🏆 20 Gy vs. ➜ no RT No RT arm stopped early due to high recurrences >20%

Results: Worse RFS with obs. 20 Gy and 36 Gy noninferior 5-yr RFS 89% vs. 84% vs. 70% 5-yr OS same at 97-100%

Interpretation: After CRu in favorable HL, RT results in superior RFS compared to observation.

NIVAHL

Hodgkin, unfavorable · Unfav: N+AVD+ISRT

Bröckelmann et al, JAMA Oncol, 2020 Bröckelmann et al, JCO, 2023 · N=109

Population: unfavorable I-IIB HL

Methods: Phase II ➜ N+AVD x4 → 30 Gy ISRT vs. ➜ N x4 → N+AVDx2 → AVD x2 → 30 Gy ISRT

Results: Interim CR after N+AVD x2 or Nx4 87% vs. 51% CR after completion of treatment 90% vs. 94% 1-yr PFS 100% vs. 98% Hypothyroid requiring long term meds in 15% LV EF wnl 95% No 2nd malignancies in either arm at median f/u 41 mos

Interpretation: Both approaches show excellent results. Single agent nivolumab upfront may warrant further investigation for de-escalation of therapy. There is a 15% chance of hypothyroidism.

HD17 GHSG

Hodgkin, unfavorable · Unfav: RT in PET negative disease IFRT vs. INRT

Borchmann et al, Lancet Oncol, 2021 Eichenauer et al, Blood, 2023 Rosenbrock et al, Int J Radiat Oncol Bio Phys 2024 · N=1100

Population: Stage I-II HL, unfavorable NLPHL allowed (excluded: stage IIB bulky or Stage IIBE)

Methods: escBEACOPP x2 + ABVD x2 → PET → ➜ Standard, non-PET directed: 30 Gy IFRT vs. ➜ PET4 directed INRT: PET4 positive refractory: 30 Gy INRT PET4 negative: no RT Noninferiority

Results: Results noninferior 5-yr PFS 97% vs. 95% 5-yr OS 98% both arms None of the failures with INRT would have been prevented by IFRT 📌 HD16 + HD17 NLPHL: PET negative NLPHL, no RT compared to RT 5-yr PFS 83% without RT, 100% with RT 5-yr OS 100% in both

Interpretation: INRT can be safely omitted in negative PET after treatment with escBEACOPP + ABVD. PET-guided INRT is noninferior to routine IFRT in PFS and OS. None of the failures with INRT would have been prevented by IFRT. RT is recommended for NLPHL in those PET negative after initial chemotherapy.

NCIC / ECOG / HD6 GHSG

Hodgkin · Unfav/Fav: STLI vs. chem

Meyer et al, NEJM, 2006 · N=405

Population: Stage IA or IIA, non-bulky

Methods: ➜ ABVD x4-6 vs. ➜ 35 Gy STLI (for unfavorable risk, ABVD x2 added before STLI) Prior to PET era

Results: Improved OS and EFS with chemotherapy, and improved FFP with RT: 12-yr OS 94% vs. 87%, p=0.04 12-yr FFP 87% vs. 92%, p=0.05 12-yr EFS 85% vs. 80% Death due to second cancers: n=4 vs. 10 Other causes (not 2nd cancer): n=2 vs. 10 Cardiac events: n=16 vs. 26 On subanalysis, the difference is in unfavorable risk but not in favorable

Interpretation: Treatment with chemo alone had superior OS compared to RT with STLI in early stage HL. With STLI 20 died of other causes (6 had chemo), leading to inferior OS.

HD14 GHSG

Hodgkin, unfavorable · Unfav: BEACOPP/ABVD vs. ABVD

von Tresckow et al, JCO, 2012 · N=1525

Population: Stage IA-IIB with at least one risk factor

Methods: ➜🏆 escBEACOPPx2 + ABVDx2 + IFRT 30 Gy vs. ➜ ABVD x4 + IFRT 30 Gy

Results: FFTF and PFS improved with BEACOPP/ABVD: 5-yr FFTF 95% escBEACOPP/ABVD vs. 88% ABVD on subanalysis, benefit only in bulky or B sx 5-yr OS 97% in both arms relapses 3% vs. 8% acute adverse effects increased, late adverse effects not different

Interpretation: BEACOPP/ABVD + IFRT improves FFTF over ABVD + IFRT in unfavorable HL.

EORTC GELA H9U

Hodgkin, unfavorable · Unfav: BEACOPP vs. ABVD

Fermé et al, Eur J Cancer, 2007 Fermé et al, Eur J Cancer, 2017 · N=808

Population: Unfavorable stage I-II HL (age ≥50, 4-5 sites, bulky, ESR ≥50 no B-symptoms, ESR ≥30 and B-symptoms)

Methods: ➜ ABVD x6 + IFRT vs. ABVD x4 + IFRT vs. ➜ BEACOPPbaseline x4 + IFRT RT to 30 Gy ±boost

Results: 5-yr EFS noninferior: 85.9% vs. 88.8% vs. 89.9% 5-yr OS 93-94% (NS) BEACOPP regimens result in worse adverse effects

Interpretation: Outcomes are noninferior. BEACOPP has more adverse effects. ABVD x4 + IFRT is the preferred regimen.

HD11 GHSG

Hodgkin, unfavorable · Unfav: BEACOPP or ABVD, IFRT dose

Eich et al, JCO, 2010 Sasse et al, JCO, 2017 · N=1395

Population: Unfavorable HL, Stage I or IIA, with ≥1 risk factor: large MS mass, extranodal disease, increase ESR, ≥3 nodal regions. Or Stage IIB with increased ESR or ≥3 nodes

Methods: ➜ escBEACOPP x4 vs. ➜ 🏆 ABVD x4 ➜ 🏆 IFRT 30 Gy vs. ➜ 20 Gy Noninferiority [prior to PET era]

Results: ABVD 30 Gy, BEACOPP 20 Gy, and BEACOPP 30 Gy are noninferior. 5-yr FFTF ~86%, 5-yr OS ~95%, 5-yr PFS ~88% ABVD 20 Gy is not noninferior. 10-yr PFS 76% ABVD 20 Gy vs. 84% in other arms BEACOPP gave increased adverse effects over ABVD: grade ≥3, 74% vs. 52% 30 Gy gave increased adverse effects over 20 Gy: grade ≥3, 12% vs. 6% No differences in secondary malignancy at 10 years

Interpretation: ABVD + 30 Gy and BEACOPP + 20 Gy are preferred treatment options for unfavorable HL. Among the arms found noninferior, these have the least adverse effects. Increased adverse effects with BEACOPP compared to ABVD. Increased adverse effects with 30 Gy compared to 20 Gy IFRT.

HD10 GHSG

Hodgkin, favorable · Fav: IFRT dose, # ABVD cycles

Engert et al, NEJM, 2010 Sasse et al, JCO, 2017 · N=1370

Population: Early-stage favorable HL (7.5% of patients had ESR<30 with B symptoms)

Methods: ➜ 🏆 ABVD x2 vs. ➜ ABVD x4 then ➜ 🏆 IFRT 20 Gy vs. ➜ 30 Gy Noninferiority [prior to PET era]

Results: No significant differences in outcomes 5-yr FFTF ~92% 10-yr OS 94% 10-yr PFS 87% No differences in secondary malignancy at 10 years

Interpretation: 20 Gy with 2 cycles ABVD is preferred in favorable HL. All regimens were noninferior, thus the most de-escalated option is preferred.

GITIL/FIL HD0607

Hodgkin, Advanced · RT benefit in bulky CR

Gallamini et al, JCO, 2018 Gallamini et al, JCO, 2020 · N=296

Population: Advanced stage with LN size ≥5 cm, s/p

Methods: Prospective, with an unpowered RT randomization ABVD x 2→ →PET2 negative Deauville 1-3→ ABVD x 6 total→ →CR PET2&6 negative and bulky size ≥5 cm: ➜ IFRT 30.6 Gy vs. ➜ obs →SER PET2 positive Deauville 4-5→ escBEACOPP (no RT) Primary endpoint: 3-yr PFS of ≥85% for entire cohort

Results: •RT vs. no RT in bulky, PET CR: 6-yr PFS 91-95%, not different Trial was not specifically powered for this randomization No difference in subgroups size 5 cm or 10 cm Median RT dose 30.6 Gy 3-yr PFS in positive PET of 60% 3-yrs PFS in negative PET of 87%

Interpretation: There was no observable difference between no RT and RT in those CR with bulky disease in advanced Hodgkin lymphoma, but this randomization was underpowered.

EORTC 20884/GPMC H34

Hodgkin, Advanced · RT after CR

Aleman et al, NEJM, 2003 · N=421

Population: Stage III-IV HL with CR or PR after MOPP-ABV

Methods: ➜ 🏆 obs vs. ➜ 16-24 Gy IFRT Refractory →🏆 18-30 Gy IFRT

Results: CR in 60% No change in 5-yr EFS or OS 5-yr EFS 77% vs. 73%, p=0.35 5-yr OS 85% vs. 78%, p=0.07 RT in refractory yielded similar outcomes to those with CR 5-yr EFS 79%, 5-yr OS 87% 5-yr 2nd malignancy 5% vs. 8% with RT, p=0.05

Interpretation: IFRT did not improve EFS or OS in advanced HL with CR. RT in refractory led to OS matching those with CR.

Hodgkin's Disease Overview Study Group, Leipzig, Germany

Hodgkin, Advanced · RT benefit

Loeffler et al, JCO, 1998 · N=1740

Population: advanced Hodgkin lymphoma

Methods: Meta-analysis of 14 trials with 16 comparison to evaluated the benefit of RT with the same chemotherapy or a different chemotherapy than in control arm

Results: -Adding RT to chemo shows 11% benefit in LC. No OS difference -There was worse OS in CR when RT was given -However in comparing the addition of RT to a second type of chemo, there is no benefit to RT, and 8% improvement in OS without RT

Interpretation: RT added to chemo leads to inferior survival if chemotherapy is given appropriately. The use of RT is limited to specific indications.

ECHELON-1

Hodgkin, Advanced · Br+AVD

Connors et al, NEJM, 2018 Ansell et al, NEJM, 2022 · N=1334

Population: Stage III-IV Hodgkin lymphoma

Methods: ➜ ABVD x6 vs. ➜ 🏆 Br+AVD (Br=brentuximab vedotin) x6 Refractory and ≥2.5 cm → 30 Gy optional (counted as PFS event)

RATHL

Hodgkin, Advanced · AVD

Luminari et al, JCO, 2023 · N=1201

Population: Advanced Hodgkin Lymphoma Stage IIB-IV or Stage IIA with bulky or ≥3 sites

Methods: Noninferiority (PFS within 5% margin) ABVDx2 → if negative PET2, randomized: ➜ ABVD x4 vs. ➜ 🏆 AVD x4 SER PET2+ → escBEACOPPx4 or BEACOPP14x6 Refractory PET6+ → 30-36 Gy RT optional

Results: Negative PET: ABVD vs. AVD 3-yr PFS 85.5% vs. 84.3%, noninferior 7-yr PFS 81.0% vs. 79.2% 7-yr 2nd malignancy 5.1% vs. 5.8% 6.6% received RT 2nd cancer was not increased with RT Positive PET, BEACOPP: 7-yr PFS 66% 2nd malignancy 2.5%

Interpretation: AVD is noninferior to ABVD for interim PET negative advanced Hodgkin's lymphoma. RT was optional for refractory.
