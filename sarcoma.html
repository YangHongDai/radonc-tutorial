🦴 SOFT TISSUE SARCOMA

軟組織肉瘤

STS 概論與關鍵證據。

Soft tissue sarcoma overview and key evidence.

處置

局部處置

肢體 STS: 廣泛切除 + RT 是保肢標準。

Neoadjuvant vs adjuvant RT (O'Sullivan, Lancet 2002): Preop 50 Gy 有較多急性 wound complication；Postop 60–66 Gy 有較多長期 fibrosis/edema。

VORTEX / NRG SARC (Wang): 減少 CTV margin 至 3 cm 效果相當。

Retroperitoneal (STRASS): preop RT 沒有整體 abdominal recurrence 好處，但可能對 liposarcoma 亞群有幫助。

Extremity STS: Wide excision + RT is limb-sparing standard.

Neo vs adjuvant RT (O'Sullivan, Lancet 2002): Preop 50 Gy — more acute wound complications; postop 60–66 Gy — more long-term fibrosis/edema.

VORTEX / NRG SARC (Wang): Reducing CTV to 3 cm gives equivalent outcomes.

Retroperitoneal (STRASS): Preop RT no overall abdominal recurrence benefit, but possible benefit in liposarcoma subgroup.

關鍵臨床試驗

以下為此主題的核心隨機試驗與樞紐研究，資料源自 Estes 2025 Key Studies 資料表，已整合於本章。

Core randomized trials and pivotal studies for this topic, from the Estes 2025 Key Studies tables, are integrated into this chapter.

RTOG 0630

Extremity · IGRT

Wang et al, JCO, 2015 Wang et al, JAMA Oncol, 2023 · N=98

Population: Extremity soft tissue sarcoma

Methods: ➜ Cohort A: Pre-op IGRT with chemotherapy (arm closed early and not reported) ➜ Cohort B: IGRT without chemotherapy IMRT or 3DCRT allowed. 50 Gy. IMRT used in 75% patients

Results: 2-yr Grade 2+ adverse effects 11% vs. 37% in historical NCIC trial. LF 6% pCR 19% LF with pCR 0% vs. 9% in <pCR 5-yr OS with pCR 100% vs. 56% if <pCR fibrosis 5% [vs. 32% in NCIC] stiffness 4% [vs. 18%] edema 5% [vs. 15%] wound complications 37% [same] lower extremity wound complications 42% [vs. 45%]

Interpretation: Pre-op RT with IGRT leads to less late adverse effects compared to historical control. pCR is associated with improved OS and reduced LF.

MSKCC

Extremity · IMRT vs. brachytherapy

Alektair et al, Cancer, 2017 · N=134

Population: Extremity soft tissue sarcoma s/p limb sparing surgery

Methods: Retrospective ➜ LDR 45 Gy vs. ➜ IMRT pre-op or post-op

Results: 5-yr LC 81% vs. 92% IMRT group had more positive and close margins, larger tumors, and nerve involvement

Interpretation: In this retrospective analysis, IMRT results in improved LC compared to LDR brachytherapy.

VORTEX

Extremity · RT volume reduction

Robinson et al, ASTRO, 2016 · N=216

Population: Extremity soft tissue sarcoma with planned surgery and post-op RT, without chemo

Methods: ➜ 50 Gy (5 cm sup/inf and 2 cm radially) + 16 Gy boost (2 cm sup/inf/radial) vs. ➜ 66 Gy (2 cm sup/inf/radial)

Results: No difference in 2-yr limb function or adverse effects (skin, subcutaneous, bone, or joint) Due to small events, not possible to assess LRFS

Interpretation: Events in both arms were low and statisitical analysis could not be be undertaken.

NCIC

Extremity · pre-op vs. post-op RT

Davis et al, Lancet, 2002 O'Sullivan et al, JCO, 2004 Davis et al, Radiother Oncol, 2005 · N=190

Population: Non-metastatic STS of limbs

Methods: ➜ pre-op 50 Gy/25 fx vs. ➜ post-op 66 Gy/33fx. Primary endpoint: wound complications (not powered for survival or recurrence) Longitudinal borders 5 cm to block edge. Boost 2 cm around target.

Results: Pre-op had increased wound complications. Post-op trended to more fibrosis, edema, stiffness. 2-yr wound adverse effects 35% pre-op vs. 17% post-op Wound complications are primarily in lower extremity, not upper LC unchanged. OS improved early in pre-op but later not different. 5-yr LC ~93%, 5-yr DM ~68% (NS) 2.5-yr OS 85% vs. 72%, p=0.04 5-yr OS 73% vs. 67% (NS) Pre-op vs. post-op 2-yr adverse effects: wound complications 35% vs. 17% (45% wound for low ext) grade 2 fibrosis 32% vs. 48%, p=0.07 edema 15% vs. 23% joint stiffness 18% vs. 23% Higher total field size in this trial than in others.

Interpretation: Pre-op and post-op RT are equally effective in LC and OS. Because adverse effects differ, anatomic site should be considered in approach.

NCI adjuvant EBRT

Extremity · post-op RT by grade

Yang et al, JCO, 1998 Beane et al, Ann Surg Oncol, 2014 · N=141

Population: Extremity sarcoma s/p WLE. Excluded gross residual tumor and widely positive margins Allowed desmoid and dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans

Methods: Low grade: ➜ 🏆 post-op RT 45 Gy, boost to 63 Gy vs. ➜ obs High grade: ➜ 🏆 post-op RT with doxo/cyclo vs. ➜ doxo/cyclo

Results: Low grade: LC 96% vs. 67% High grade: LC 100% vs. 81% Low grade excluding desmoid and dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans: LC 95% vs. 68%, p=0.067 (low power) No difference in OS or DMFS 10-yr OS 77% RT vs. 82% (p=.22, all grades) 20-yr OS 61% vs. 71%, not different EBRT had more wound complications, edema, and limb deficits

Interpretation: EBRT provides high rates of local control. When evaluating low risk tumors while excluding desmoid and dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans, there is trend to benefit with radiation that is not signficant, however the analysis is likely underpowered.

MDACC

Extremity · S alone and selective RT

Pisters et al, Ann Surg, 2007 · N=58

Population: Soft tissue sarcoma T1 (size <5 cm), trunk or extremity

Methods: Prospective Function sparing surgery→ ➜ If R1→RT ➜ If R0→obs

Results: R0, obs: 5-yr LR 8%, 10-yr LR 11% R1, radiation: LR 43% 58% were high grade. 11/12 recurrences were in high grade, and 8/12 recurrences were deep tumors

Interpretation: Findings suggest that surgery alone is acceptable for some patients such as those of size <5 cm with complete resection, grade 1-2.

MSKCC

Extremity · post-op brachytherapy

Pisters et al, JCO, 1996 · N=160

Population: Extremity and superficial trunk sarcoma s/p WLE with goal of complete resection (15% R1, 85% R0)

Methods: ➜ 🏆 brachy Ir-192 42-45 Gy+ 2cm margin vs. ➜ obs

Results: 5-yr LC improved with BT: 82% vs. 69% obs high grade 5-yr LC 89% vs. 66% low grade no difference in negative margins, 13% vs. 28% in positive margins, 33% vs. 36% No change in DM (17% vs. 24%) or DFS (84% vs. 81%) 15% were R1

Interpretation: Adjuvant brachy improves LC for high grade STS. No difference in DSS or DM.

NCI Limb sparing

Extremity · Amputation vs. WLE and RT

Rosenberg et al, Ann Surg, 1982 · N=43

Population: High-grade extremity STS

Methods: ➜ WLE + post-op 50 Gy + 10-20 Gy boost vs. ➜ amputation adjuvant chemo in both arms

Results: LR 15% vs. 0% amputation No significant difference in 5-yr DFS (71% vs. 78%) or OS (83% vs. 88%)

Interpretation: Limb-sparing surgery with RT and chemo is an effective treatment strategy.

HYPORT-STS

Extremity · hypofrac

Guadagnolo et al, Lancet Oncol, 2022 · N=120

Population: Extremity or trunk soft tissue sarcoma

Methods: Phase II ➜ Pre-op 42.75 Gy/ 15 fx daily

Results: 30-month LC 93% Major wound complications within 120 days 31% Grade ≥3 adverse effects in n=4

Interpretation: Hypofractionated radiation leads to favorable local control with acceptable adverse effects in this cohort.

DOREMY

Extremity · hypofrac

Lansu et al, JAMA Oncol, 2020 Lansu et al, JAMA Oncol, 2026 · N=79

Population: Localized myxoid liposarcoma, trunk or extremity

Methods: Prospective. ➜ Pre-op 36 Gy/ 18 fx daily Surgery at ≥4 weeks

Results: 91% had a ≥50% pathologic response LC 100% 5-yr LFRFS 97% 5-yr PFS 81% 5-yr DSS 90% 5-yr OS 89% Wound complicatoin 16% Grade 2 adverse effects 15% Grade 3 adverse effects 3%

Interpretation: This de-escalated regimen for myxoid sarcoma has excellent LC and pathologic response.

Maria Sklodowska- Curie Memorial Cancer Center, Warsaw, Poland

Extremity · hypofrac

Koseła-Paterczyk et al, Eur J Surg Oncol, 2014 · N=272

Population: Extremity or trunk soft tissue sarcoma

Methods: Prospective. ➜ Pre-op 25 Gy/ 5 fx daily Immediate surgery

Results: 3-yr LR 19% 3-yr OS 72% 42% had adverse effects 7% surgery for complications 65% were grade 3, 42% with size >10 cm

Interpretation: Hypofractionated radiation leads to favorable local control with acceptable adverse effects in this cohort.

Fondazione IRCCS Istituto Nazionale dei Tumori, Milan, Italy

Extremity · Nomogram for OS and DM

Callegaro et al, Lancet Oncol, 2016 · N=1452

Population: Extremity soft tissue sarcoma

Methods: Development of nomogram Initial cohort formed at Milan and then externally validated at London, Toronto, and Gustave Roussy.

Results: Refer to publication for useful nomogram to predict OS and DM Overall 10-yr OS 79%, crude DM 25% Factors include age, size, grade, histology

Interpretation: Nomogram was developed to predict OS and DM in sarcoma of the extremity and externally validated.

MSKCC

Extremity · Nomogram for LR

Cahlon et al, Ann Surg, 2012 · N=684

Population: Extremity soft tissue sarcoma, nonmetastatic, prospective database 1982-2006, receiving no RT or chemo

Methods: Development of nomogram to predict LR. Internally validated using concordance index, 10-fold cross validation, and comparison to observed LR

Results: Refer to publication for useful nomogram to predict LR. Factors include age, size, margin, grade, and histology. Histology other than atypical lipoma or grade 1 has the greatest effect.

Interpretation: Nomogram was developed to predict LR in sarcoma of the extremity and validated internally.

STRASS

Retroperitoneal · pre-op RT vs. no RT

Bonvalot et al, Lancet Oncol, 2020 Haas et al, Cancer, 2022 · N=266

Population: operable localized retroperitoneal soft tissue sarcoma Exclusion: expected R2 (subtype characteristics: 33% well differentiated LPS, 39% dedifferentiated LPS, 2% other LPS, 14% leiomyosarcoma, 11% other)

Methods: ➜ pre-op RT to 50.4 Gy then en bloc surgery in 4-8 weeks vs. ➜ en bloc surgery Primary endpoint: abdominal RFS (composite of LR or DM progression during or after RT based on CT 2 weeks after RT, 3 on ASA scale, R2, sarcomatosis, or LR following R0/R1 resection) Dose constraints and margins: https://acsjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/action/downloadSupplement?doi=10.1002%2Fcncr.34239&file=cncr34239-sup-0001-TableS1-S6.pdf

Results: No difference in abdominal RFS, OS, DM 3-yr abdominal RFS ~60% Median abd RFS 4.5-5.0 yrs 5-yr OS 80%, DMFS 68% both arms No change in operability, re-operations -various sensitivity analyses found no differences in abd RFS -in subanalysis of liposarcoma, CI crosses to 1.02 and p not provided, but curves appear to begin to separate. Trial not powered for subtypes Selected sub-endpoints of abd RFS: -LR following R0/R1: 13% vs. 29% -LR/DM progression during/after RT 14% (12% were LR, 2% DM) (supp p6-7, calculated by myself) -Serious adverse effects 24% vs. 10% -In RT group, lymphopenia in 77%, a…

Interpretation: Pre-op RT in RP sarcoma did not improve abdominal RFS. There may be benefit in the sub-endpoint of LR following R0/R1. The trial was not powered to evaluate specific subtypes. RT QA noncompliance was associated with worse OS.

STRASS/STREXIT

Retroperitoneal · pre-op RT vs. no RT

Callegaro et al, Ann Surg, 2022 · N=408

Population: "STREXIT" cohort: Sarcoma patients at 10 STRASS recruiting centers that were not enrolled in STRASS, s/p curative intent surgery, with or without RT

Methods: Propensity matched analysis of STRASS and STREXIT cohorts ➜ pre-op RT to 50.4 Gy then en bloc surgery in 4-8 weeks vs. ➜ en bloc surgery Endpoint: abdominal RFS

Results: RT improved abdominal RFS in well-differentiated liposarcoma and grade 1-2 dedifferentiated liposarcoma No improvement in abdominal RFS with RT in grade 3 dedifferentiated liposarcoma or leiomyosarcoma

Interpretation: In this propensity matched analysis, pre-op RT was associated with improved abdominal RFS in well-differentiated liposarcoma and grade 1-2 dedifferentiated liposarcoma. A randomized trial focused on this subgroup may be warranted.

STRASS II (protocol)

Retroperitoneal · pre-op chemo vs. no chemo

EORTC, open protocol · N=250

Population: operable localized retroperitoneal soft tissue sarcoma Exclusion: expected R2

Methods: ➜ pre-op chemo x3 then en bloc surgery in 3-6 weeks vs. ➜ en bloc surgery High grade LPS: doxo + ifos Leiomyosarcoma: doxo + dacarbazing

Results: ongoing study Primary endpoint: DFS Secondary endpoint: RFS, OS, LF, DM

Brigham and Women's Hospital, Harvard Medical School

Retroperitoneal · Nomogram

Raut et al, Cancer, 2016 · N=1154

Population: RPS patients undergoing definitive resection

Methods: Nomogram developed and then subsequently validated at North American and European centers

Results: Refer to publication for useful nomogram 7-yr DFS 38%, 7-yr OS 58% overall 30% received RT Prognostic factors: age, tumor size, grade, histologic subtype, multifocality, and quality of surgery Liposarcoma was worse tumor type

Interpretation: Nomogram was developed to predict DFS and OS in RP sarcoma and then externally validated.

Fondazione IRCCS Istituto Nazionale dei Tumori, Milan, Italy

Retroperitoneal · Factors for recurrence

Gronchi et al, Ann Surg, 2016 · N=1007

Population: RPS at 6 European and two North American institutions

Methods: Retrospective review of outcomes, treatment modality, and factors for recurrence

Results: -Factors affecting outcomes: size, grade, subtype, multifocality, degree of resection -refer to figures for outcomes by subtype -RT impacted LR in liposarcoma but not leiomyosarcoma -Well differentiated liposarcoma: 8-yr LR 5% with RT + extended surgery, 35% with extended surgery + no RT, 42% limited surgery and no RT

Interpretation: Outcomes in RPS are outlined based on patient characteristics. There may be a benefit with radiation therapy in well differetiated liposarcoma.

MGH Cancer Center

Spine chordoma · Dose escalation

DeLaney et al, J Surg Oncol, 2014 · N=50

Population: primary or locally recurrent T, L, S, or paraspinal chordomas or sarcomas

Methods: Phase II Dose escalation with photons or protons ≤72.0 GyRBE in n=25 76.6-77.4 GyRBE in n=25

Results: Primary tumors 5-yr LC 94%, 8-yr LC 85% All tumors 5-yr LC 81%, 8-yr LC 74% 8-yr grade 3-4 adverse effects 13% -No myelopathies. Sacral neuropathies in n=3 with 76.6-77.4 GyRBE

Interpretation: Dose escalation for spinal sarcomas results in favorable control and acceptable adverse effects.

Ludwig Maximilian University Hospital, Munich, Germany

General · Benefit of RT

Albertsmeier et al, Ann Surg Oncol, 2018 · N=3958

Population: resectable soft tissue sarcomas, any site

Methods: Meta-analysis of studies evaluating EBRT vs. no EBRT (not all randomized)

Results: -RT reduced LR in RPS and other sites -RT improved OS in RPS but not others -Pre-op RT improved LR over post-op -Wound complications more in pre-op RT

Interpretation: There is LC benefit with adjuvant RT. There may be an OS benefit with RT in RP sarcoma.

EORTC 62931

General · Adjuvant chemotherapy

DeLaney et al, J Surg Oncol, 2014 · N=351

Population: resected soft tissue sarcoma, RT optional

Methods: doxorubicin/ifosphamide vs. no chemo

Results: No OS or DM benefit with chemo

Interpretation: There is no OS or DM benefit with adjuvant doxo/ifosphamide.

University of Waterloo, Ontario, Canada

General · Adjuvant chemotherapy

Pervaiz et al, Cancer, 2008 · N=1953

Population: 18 trials of adj chemo for adults

Methods: Meta-analysis evaluating phase III adjuvant chemotherapy trials

Results: Absolute benefits for doxorubicin alone: LC 5%, DM 10%, RFS 10%, and OS NS There was OS benefit for doxo+ifosfamide

Interpretation: There is OS benefit with adjuvant doxo/ifosphamide in this meta-analysis.

IRCCS Humanitas Research Hospital, Rozzano, Milan, Italy.

Oligometastasis · SBRT

Navarria et al, IJROBP, 2022 · N=44

Population: ≤4 lung metastases, ≤5 cm, from sarcoma

Methods: Phase II peripheral ≤10mm: 30 Gy/ 1 fx peripheral 11-20mm: 60 Gy/ 3 fx peripheral >20mm: 48 Gy/ 4 fx central:60 Gy/ 8 fx

Results: 1-yr LC 99% 1/2/3/4/5-yr PFS 50%/20%/12%/12%/12%

Interpretation: SBRT for sarcoma lung metastasis leads to excellent local control. Randomized trials are warranted to compare this approach to surgery. Long term PFS is still poor however, and investigation into additional therapy is warranted.
