總論

Skin cancer 總論

Skin cancer 是非常常見的癌症。美國約 1 in 5 的人到 70 歲前會得到皮膚癌，每天約 9500 人被診斷為 skin cancer。最常見的是 non-melanomatous skin cancers，佔 96–99%，其中 BCC 約 80%、cSCC 約 20%。Melanoma 約佔 1–4%。其他較少見皮膚癌包括 Merkel cell carcinoma, MCC，以及 cutaneous lymphoma，例如 mycosis fungoides。

Skin cancer is very common. In the United States, approximately 1 in 5 people develop skin cancer by age 70, and about 9,500 people are diagnosed with skin cancer each day. Non-melanomatous skin cancers account for 96–99% of cases; among these, BCC accounts for about 80% and cSCC for about 20%. Melanoma accounts for about 1–4%. Other rare skin cancers include Merkel cell carcinoma and cutaneous lymphoma such as mycosis fungoides.

皮膚解剖與腫瘤來源

皮膚分為三大層：epidermis、dermis、hypodermis / subcutaneous tissue。Epidermis 由外到內分為 stratum corneum、lucidum、granulosum、spinosum、basale。BCC 源自 basal layer 相關細胞；cSCC 源自 squamous / keratinocyte lineage；melanoma 源自 melanocyte；MCC 與 Merkel cell / neuroendocrine lineage 相關。

The skin has three major layers: epidermis, dermis, and hypodermis / subcutaneous tissue. From superficial to deep, the epidermis contains the stratum corneum, lucidum, granulosum, spinosum, and basale. BCC arises from basal-layer related cells; cSCC from the squamous / keratinocyte lineage; melanoma from melanocytes; and MCC from the Merkel cell / neuroendocrine lineage.

Risk factors 與 prevention

皮膚癌最重要的危險因子是 年齡與 UV exposure。UVB 波長約 290–320 nm，與 sunburn 和 DNA damage 關係密切；UVA 波長約 320–400 nm，與 photoaging 相關。記憶法：UVB = Burning，UVA = Aging。

遺傳 syndrome：Gorlin syndrome / PTCH gene 與 BCC 相關；CDKN2A、CDK4、XP、BRCA2 與 melanoma 相關；MAGT1、ATM、BRCA1/BRCA2、TP53 與 MCC 相關。

Prevention 核心是 sun protection：避免曬傷與 tanning beds、10 AM–4 PM 尋找 shade、戴寬邊帽與 sunglasses、穿防曬衣、使用 broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen 並定時補擦。

The most important skin cancer risk factors are age and UV exposure. UVB has a wavelength of about 290–320 nm and is closely linked to sunburn and DNA damage. UVA has a wavelength of about 320–400 nm and is linked to photoaging. Memory aid: UVB = Burning; UVA = Aging.

Prevention centers on sun protection: avoid sunburn and tanning beds, seek shade from 10 AM to 4 PM, wear a wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses, use sun-protective clothing, and apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen regularly.

Workup

Workup：H&P、pathology、SLNB 與 imaging

Workup 從完整 H&P 開始，包括 prior skin cancer、prior treatments、history of RT、immunosuppression、transplant history、phototherapy、chemical exposure。身體檢查要做 complete skin exam，尋找 skip lesions，並檢查 regional nodes。Head and neck skin cancers 要做 neurological exam，以排除 PNI。

Pathology 可用 punch、shave、excisional 或 incisional biopsy。Melanoma 需要記錄 Breslow depth、ulceration、mitotic rate、margins、regression、microsatellites；melanoma 需做 molecular testing，尤其 BRAF V600 mutation。

CancerSLNB indication
MelanomaBreslow depth >0.8 mm、ulceration，或 risk factors，例如 age ≥42 years
MCCAlways consider / usually always SLNB
cSCCRecurrent 或 very-high-risk 可考慮
BCC極少 nodal spread，只有 very high-risk 或 clinical concern 才評估 nodes
Imaging：懷疑 PNI、skull base involvement、periorbital involvement，或 lesion fixed to muscle/bone/fascia → MRI with and without contrast。Locally advanced tumor 或 regional lymphadenopathy → contrast CT。High-risk 或 node-positive patients → FDG-PET/CT 排除 distant metastasis。

Workup begins with full history and physical examination, including prior skin cancer, prior treatments, history of RT, immunosuppression, transplant history, phototherapy, and chemical exposure. Examination requires a complete skin exam, assessment for skip lesions, and regional nodal examination. Head and neck skin cancers require neurologic examination to evaluate for PNI.

Pathology can be obtained by punch, shave, excisional, or incisional biopsy. Melanoma pathology must report Breslow depth, ulceration, mitotic rate, margins, regression, and microsatellites. Melanoma also needs molecular testing, especially BRAF V600 mutation.

治療架構

治療總架構、resection margins 與 lymph node management

低風險 BCC / cSCC 可用 excision、Mohs 或 alternate focal therapy。Very superficial 或 in situ disease 可考慮 electrodessication & curettage、cryotherapy、superficial radiotherapy、topical 5-FU、imiquimod。

高風險 / very-high-risk BCC 或 cSCC 若 resectable，標準是 WLE 或 Mohs surgery。Adjuvant RT 高風險因素包括 positive lymph nodes、positive margins、vascular invasion、extensive PNI 或 large nerve involvement >0.1 mm、deep tissue invasion、primary tumor >4 cm、recurrent disease。Unresectable disease 可做 definitive RT 或 systemic therapy。

Low-risk BCC / cSCC can be treated with excision, Mohs, or alternate focal therapy. Very superficial or in situ disease may be treated with electrodessication and curettage, cryotherapy, superficial radiotherapy, topical 5-FU, or imiquimod.

High-risk / very-high-risk BCC or cSCC, if resectable, is treated with WLE or Mohs surgery. Adjuvant RT risk factors include positive lymph nodes, positive margins, vascular invasion, extensive PNI or large-nerve involvement >0.1 mm, deep tissue invasion, primary tumor >4 cm, and recurrent disease. Unresectable disease may receive definitive RT or systemic therapy.

One-sentence summary: Skin cancer treatment depends on histology + location + risk stratification. Low-risk BCC/cSCC can use local therapy or simple excision; high-risk cSCC, especially PNI, LN+, deep invasion, large/recurrent disease, needs aggressive surgery ± adjuvant RT; definitive RT is important when surgery is not feasible or when function/cosmesis preservation matters; advanced cSCC is now in the PD-1 inhibitor era, advanced BCC uses SHH inhibition, and melanoma/MCC depend heavily on SLNB, immunotherapy, and risk-adapted RT.

關鍵臨床試驗

以下為此主題的核心隨機試驗與樞紐研究，資料源自 Estes 2025 Key Studies 資料表，已整合於本章。

Core randomized trials and pivotal studies for this topic, from the Estes 2025 Key Studies tables, are integrated into this chapter.

ANZMTG 01.02/ TROG 02.01

Melanoma · Benefit of RT to LN

Burmeister et al, Lancet, 2012 Henderson et al, Lancet, 2015 · N=217

Population: s/p LN dissection for clinically palpable nodes [SLN detected only not eligible] Must be node positive in ≥1 LN parotid, ≥2 LNs cervical or axilla , ≥3 groin, ECE, ≥3 cm LN cervical, or ≥4 cm LN axilla/groin

Methods: ➜ 48 Gy/20 fx vs. ➜ obs Primary endpoint: LN field relapse as first relapse and late adverse effects Secondary endpoints: adjacent relapse, DM, OS, PFS, time to infield progression

Results: RT improved LN relapse 6-yr LN relapse 21% vs. 36% RFS and OS unchanged, 5-yr OS 35% 22% with RT had grade 3-4 adverse effects, 20% grade 3 (skin in 10% and subcutaneous tissue in 7%). 7.7% with increased limb volume in RT group

Interpretation: Adjuvant radiation therapy for clinically detected LNs in melanoma improves LN relapse but has no confirmed benefit in RFS or OS. Immunotherapy, clinical trials, or observation are alternative treatment options that may be more ideal.

MDACC

Melanoma · RT fractionation

Ang et al, IJROBP, 1994 · N=174

Population: High-risk melanoma of H&N: 1) size ≥1.5 mm or Clark's level >IV, N0 2) palpable nodes 3) nodal relapse

Methods: Phase II: ➜ 30 Gy/5 fx biweekly 95% dose to 95% volume

Results: 5-yr LRC 88%, 5-yr OS 47%. Late adverse effects in 3 pts: neck fibrosis, mild hearing loss, exposure of EAC cartilage

Interpretation: 5 fraction adjuvant RT led to favorable LRC and low adverse effects.

RTOG 8305

Melanoma · RT fractionation

Sause et al, IJROBP, 1991 · N=126

Population: Melanoma patients with gross disease

Methods: ➜ 32 Gy/ 4 fx (1 fx weekly) vs. ➜ 50 Gy/ 20 fx (5 days per week)

Results: CR 24%, PR 35%. No difference between arms.

Interpretation: The two regimens each showed favorable outcomes with no difference between arms.

NCDB

Melanoma · Benefit of RT to LN

Danish et al, Melanoma Res, 2016 · N=912

Population: Melanoma patients, pathologically node+, s/p surgery to regional nodes, Stage IIIA-C, 2003-2011

Methods: NCDB retrospective review. Compared OS in RT to regional nodes vs. no RT. Propensity score matching used.

Results: No difference in OS with regional node RT (HR 1.09, p=0.640)

Interpretation: Adjuvant RT to regional nodes in node positive melanoma did not lead to improvement in OS in this NCDB analysis. Randomized trials are required for confirmation as adjustments for imbalances in database studies cannot remove all confounding variables.

MDACC

Melanoma · RT outcomes and risk factors

Ballo et al, IJROBP, 2006 · N=466

Population: Node+ melanoma s/p LND and RT

Methods: Retrospective review of outcomes

Results: 5-yr rates: DSS 49%, DFS 42%, DMFS 44%, RC 89% 10-yr lymphedema 11% overall, 27% in groin RT recommended for: Most patients: ≥4 LN, LN size ≥3 cm, ECE, recurrence Groin: consider if Groin: 2 high risk features ≥2 high risk features, esp ECE Cervical: smaller of less LNs, ECE

Interpretation: Surgery and adjuvant RT did lead to good regional control, but the risk of DM remains high.

MSLT-I

Melanoma · Lymph node dissection

Morton et al, NEJM, 2014 · N=2001

Population: primary cutaneous melanoma

Methods: WLE and nodal observation with dissection for nodal relapse vs. WLE and SLNB with immediate node dissection if nodes detected

Results: No difference in CSS overall Int depth 10-yr DFS 65% vs. 71%, p=0.01 Thick depth 10-yr DFS 41% vs. 51% Int depth 10-yr DMFS and CSS improved

Interpretation: SLN management improves DFS in all melanoma patients. DMFS and CSS are also improved in intermediate thickness.

MSLT-II

Melanoma · Lymph node dissection

Faries et al, NEJM, 2017 · N=1934

Population: intermediate thickness melanoma (1.2-3.5 mm) with positive SLN biopsy

Methods: ➜ Completion LND vs. ➜ obs with US Primary endpoint: melanoma specific survival

Results: Per protocol analysis: 3-yr CSS 86%, not different -Subgroup analysis showed no survival benefit in any group including by depth, # nodes, node size, site, ulceration 3-yr DM no difference 3-yr DFS 68% dissection vs. 63%, p=0.05 3-yr node control 92% vs. 77%, SS -Non-sentinal nodes predicted recurrence, HR 1.78 -"Adjuvant therapy" given in 8.1% vs. 6.5% (no details on how many had radiation)

Interpretation: LND improved regional control and provided prognostic information but had no benefit in CSS.

DeCOG-SLT

Melanoma · Lymph node dissection

Leiter et al, JCO, 2019 · N=473

Population: SLN+ melanoma

Methods: ➜ Completion LND vs. ➜ obs Primary endpoint: DMFS, RFS, OS

Results: No difference in DMFS, RFS, OS 5-yr DMFS ~67%, p=0.87 5-yr DMFS was worse with increased tumor load but still did not differ between arms

Interpretation: LND had no benefit in DM or OS.

ABC

Melanoma · Ipilimumab plus nivolumab in brain mets

Long et al, Lancet Oncol, 2025 · N=89

Population: Melanoma with brain metastasis

Methods: Phase II ➜ Ipilimumab plus nivolumab vs. ➜ nivolumab Cohort C (nonrandomized, prior brain-directed therapy, neuro symptoms, or leptomeningeal) →nivolumab

Results: Intracranial ORR 51% vs. 20% vs. 6% 7-yr intracranial PFS 42% vs. 15% vs. 6% 7-yr OS 48% vs. 26% vs. 13%

Interpretation: Ipilimumab plus nivolumab, without radiation, offers some disease control in melanoma brain metastasis.

NADINA

Melanoma · Neoadjuvant immunotherapy

Blank et al, NEJM, 2024 · N=423

Population: resectable Stage III melanoma

Methods: Phase III ➜ 🏆 neoadjuvant ipilimumab plus nivolumab x2 cycles vs. ➜ adjuvant nivolumab x 12 cycles

Results: 12-mos EFS 84% vs. 57% With neoadjuvant: Major path response 59%, 8% PR, 26% non response, 2% progression 12-mos RFS was 95% in major path response, 76% in PR, 57% in nonresponse 4% in the neoadjuvant group did not undergo surgery

Interpretation: Neoadjuvant ipilimumab plus nivolumab improves EFS compared to adjuvant only nivolumab.

S1801

Melanoma · Neoadjuvant immunotherapy

Patel et al, NEJM, 2023 · N=213

Population: resectable Stage III melanoma

Methods: Phase II randomized ➜ 🏆 neoadjuvant pembrolizumab x3 cycles → surgery → adjuvant pembrolizumab x15 cycles vs. ➜ adjuvant pembrolizumab x 18 cycles

Results: 2-yr EFS 72% vs. 49 21% pCR in neoadjuvant

Interpretation: Neoadjuvant & adjuvant pembrolizumab resulted in improved EFS compared to adjuvant only pembrolizumab.

EORTC

Melanoma · Adjuvant ipilimumab

Eggermont et al, NEJM, 2016 · N=951

Population: resected Stage III melanoma

Methods: ➜ 🏆 adjuvant ipilimumab vs. ➜ placebo

Results: 5-yr RFS 40.8% vs 30.3% 5-yr OS 65.4% vs 54.4% 5-yr DMFS 48.3% vs 38.9% Grade 3-4 AE 54.1% vs 26.2% Immune related AE Grade 3-4 41.6% vs 2.7% In ipilimumab group 1.1% died due to immune related AE

Interpretation: Ipilimumab results in improved OS, RFS, and DMFS in resected Stage III melanoma.

EORTC Keynote 054

Melanoma · Adjuvant pembrolizumab

Eggermont et al, NEJM, 2018 Eggermont et al, NEJM, 2021 · N=1019

Population: resected Stage III melanoma

Methods: ➜ 🏆 adjuvant pembrolizumab vs. ➜ placebo

Results: 3.5-yr DMFS 65% vs. 49% 3.5-yr RFS 60% vs. 41% If, PDL1+, 3.5-yr RFS 61% vs. 44% Grade 3-5 adverse effects 15% vs. 3%

Interpretation: Pembrolizumab improved DMFS and RFS in resected Stage III melanoma.

CheckMate 238

Melanoma · Adjuvant nivolumab vs. ipilimumab

Weber et al, NEJM, 2017 · N=906

Population: resected Stage IIIB, IIIC, or IV melanoma

Methods: ➜ 🏆 nivolumab vs. ➜ ipilimumab

Results: 1-yr RFS 71% nivo vs. 61% ipi Grade 3-4 adverse events 14% vs. 46% D/c due to adverse effects 10% vs. 43% Deaths due to adverse effects within 100 days: n=0 vs. n=2

Interpretation: Nivolumab gave longer RFS and less grade 3-4 effects than ipilimumab for resected Stage IIIB-IV melanoma.

COMBO-AD

Melanoma · Adjuvant dabrafenib + trametinib

Long et al, NEJM, 2017 Dummer et al, NEJM, 2020 · N=870

Population: resected Stage III melanoma with BRAF V600E or V600K mutations

Methods: ➜ 🏆 adjuvant dabrafenib + trametinib vs. ➜ placebos

Results: Improved RFS with combo pill 3-yr RFS 58% vs. 38% 5-yr RFS 52% vs. 36% 3-yr OS 86% vs. 77% 5-yr DMFS 65% vs. 54%

Interpretation: Adjuvant dabrafenib plus trametinib improved RFS in BRAF mutated Stage III melanoma.

Gustave Roussy, Villejuif, France

Basal cell carcinoma · surgery vs. definitive RT

Avril et al, Br J Cancer, 1997 · N=347

Population: BCC of face measuring less <4cm

Methods: Randomized: surgery vs. RT In RT group, 55% had interstitial brachy, 33% contact therapy, and 12% conventional

Results: 4-yr failure 0.7% surgery and 7.5% RT, p=0.003 Good cosmetic results per patient 87% surgery vs 69% RT

Interpretation: In BCC <4 cm, surgery has improved LC compared to radiation. Radiation also shows favorable results and is a reasonable treatment alternative.

Fox Chase Cancer Center, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

BCC/SCC · Mohs, BT, EBRT

Lee et al, Cancer, 2019 · N=18,095

Population: BCC and SCC

Methods: Meta-analysis of reports of outcomes of brachytherapy, Mohs, EBRT, and conventional excision

Results: good cosmesis in 98% BT, 96% Mohs (1 study only), 75% EBRT, 81%, CE 1-yr LR 0% BT, 0.2% Mohs, 2% EBRT, 0.8% CE

Interpretation: There is excellent LC and good cosmesis with both BT and Mohs. Selection bias may be present in these studies.

Dermatology Associates of Tallahassee, Florida

BCC/SCC · Definitive RT

Cognetta et al, J Am Acad Dermatol, 2012 · N=1715

Population: 712 BCC and 994 SCC (861 in situ and 133 invasive SCC)

Methods: Retrospective. Superficial xray

Results: 5-yr control 96% BCC and 94% SCC Predictors of recurrence: size ≥2 cm, male

Interpretation: Superficial RT for BCC/SCC leads to favorable LC.

Universidad de Las Palmas de Gran Canaria, Spain

BCC/SCC · Definitive RT

Hernández-Machin et al, Int J Dermatolol, 2007 · N=800

Population: 604 BCC and 106 SCC

Methods: Retrospective. Superficial xray

Results: 5-yr LC 94% BCC and 93% SCC 15-yr cure 85% BCC and 78% SCC Predictors of BCC recurrence: nose and size ≥10 mm

British Columbia

BCC/SCC · Definitive RT

Kwan et al, IJROBP, 2004 · N=182

Population: 61 BCC and 121 SCC.

Methods: Retrospective: Orthovoltage, electrons, MV photons and fractionation at oncologist discretion

Results: 4-yr LRC 86% for BCC and 58% for SCC.

Interpretation: Orthovoltage RT for BCC/SCC leads to favorable LC. BCC have better outcome than SCC.

Washington University

BCC/SCC · Definitive RT

Locke et al, IJROBP, 2001 · N=468

Population: 389 BCC and 142 SCC, including recurrent

Methods: Retrospective: Mostly electrons, superficial X-rays, or both. <2% MV photons.

Results: BCC: 92% overall control. SCC: 80% overall control. Larger size, smaller fraction size, recurrent tumors correlated with worse outcomes.

Interpretation: RT for BCC/SCC leads to favorable LC. BCC have better outcome than SCC.
