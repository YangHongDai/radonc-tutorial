緩和性放射治療與放射腫瘤急症共通原則

第一部分：緩和性放射治療與放射腫瘤急症共通原則

一、最重要的急症原則

放射腫瘤急症不等於一定要立刻開始放射治療。首先必須判斷病人是否真正處於立即危險、是否需要組織診斷、是否曾接受同部位放射治療，以及是否應先接受手術或介入處置。

重要原則可概括為：「腫瘤只是傳聞，組織才是關鍵。」除非生命徵象或神經功能正在快速惡化，否則應盡量取得病理，因淋巴瘤、肉瘤、非小細胞肺癌（NSCLC）、小細胞肺癌（SCLC）與生殖細胞腫瘤對化學治療、類固醇與放射治療（RT）的敏感度及治療順序完全不同。若必須進行急診放射治療，應清楚記錄病理狀態。

若考慮再次照射，需確認過去是否照射相同區域、調閱既往治療計畫，盡可能進行治療計畫加總並記錄危及器官（OAR）累積劑量，並親自進行床邊評估。

Part I. Shared principles for palliative radiotherapy and radiation emergencies

1. The most important emergency rule

A radiation emergency does not automatically mean immediate radiotherapy. The first step is to decide whether the situation is truly urgent, whether tissue diagnosis is needed, whether the patient has had prior radiotherapy, and whether surgery or an interventional procedure should come first.

The teaching phrase is: “Tumor is the rumor, but tissue is the issue.” Unless vital signs or neurologic function are rapidly deteriorating, pathology should be confirmed whenever possible, because lymphoma, sarcoma, NSCLC, SCLC, and germ-cell tumor differ substantially in their response to chemotherapy, steroids, radiotherapy, and treatment sequencing. If emergency radiotherapy is delivered, document the pathology status. For re-irradiation, review the prior records, ask whether the same region was irradiated, perform plan summation when possible, document organ-at-risk dose, and assess the patient at the bedside.

上腔靜脈症候群：病理生理、臨床表現、評估、嚴重度與治療

第二部分：上腔靜脈症候群

一、病理生理

上腔靜脈症候群（SVC syndrome）是上腔靜脈受到外部壓迫或內部阻塞，造成頭頸部、上肢與上胸部靜脈回流受阻，引起近端靜脈鬱血。上腔靜脈負責約全身三分之一的靜脈回流，收集頭部、上肢與上半身血液；左右頭臂靜脈匯合形成上腔靜脈，最後進入右心房。側枝循環可經奇靜脈、內胸靜脈、椎靜脈、外側胸靜脈、椎旁靜脈與食道靜脈建立。

二、臨床表現

常見症狀與徵象包括顏面水腫、上肢水腫、頸靜脈怒張、胸壁靜脈擴張、顏面潮紅、呼吸困難、咳嗽、聲音沙啞、喘鳴、昏厥、頭痛、頭暈、意識混亂與嗜睡。常見比例約為：顏面水腫 82%、頸靜脈怒張 63%、呼吸困難／咳嗽 54%、胸壁靜脈擴張 53%、上肢水腫 46%。喘鳴、意識混亂或嗜睡雖較少見，但提示可能有危及生命的氣道或神經學受損。

三、常見原因

過去約 90% 的 SVC syndrome 由惡性腫瘤造成；隨著心律調節器導線與中心靜脈導管使用增加，目前惡性腫瘤約占 60%。非惡性原因包括導管相關血栓、慢性縱膈炎、胸骨後甲狀腺腫、鬱血性心衰竭與主動脈瘤。

惡性原因以NSCLC 約 50%最常見，其次為SCLC 約 25%；SCLC 因中央型腫瘤生長快速，更容易出現急性症狀。非何杰金淋巴瘤（NHL）約占 12%，並是年齡 <50 歲病人的重要原因，常見病理包括瀰漫性大 B 細胞淋巴瘤（DLBCL）、淋巴母細胞淋巴瘤與原發性縱膈 B 細胞淋巴瘤。其他原因包括轉移癌、生殖細胞腫瘤、胸腺瘤與間皮瘤。

四、檢查評估

評估首先確認症狀持續時間與嚴重度、呼吸狀態、神經學狀態及血流動力學穩定性。病史需詢問既往癌症與血管內處置，可利用駕照照片或家屬描述比較顏面變化。影像包括胸部 X 光評估縱膈腔增寬，以及顯影胸部電腦斷層（CT）確認阻塞與側枝循環。實驗室檢查包括基本代謝檢查與全血球計數；若原發癌未知，應檢測乳酸脫氫酶（LDH）、甲型胎兒蛋白（AFP）與 β-hCG。

外壓型病灶通常需要組織診斷，可依情況使用支氣管鏡／支氣管內超音波（EBUS）、縱膈腔鏡、胸腔穿刺或胸腔鏡手術（VATS）。

六、治療

初始處置包括抬高床頭、補充氧氣；若有腦水腫，可使用 dexamethasone 或利尿劑；若有血栓，應給予抗凝治療。若存在呼吸、神經學或血流動力學受損，血管內支架為第一線處置，因症狀緩解最快。

若未立即危及生命，抗癌治療前應先取得病理，因放射治療與類固醇可能影響組織學診斷。後續依病理選擇治療：淋巴瘤、SCLC 與生殖細胞腫瘤通常對化學治療反應快速；SCLC／NSCLC 可使用放射治療；胸腺瘤可考慮手術。

七、放射治療角色

放射治療可在約 72 小時內改善症狀，尤其適用於放射敏感腫瘤。若治療意圖仍為根治，可先使用3–4 Gy／次，連續 2–3 天快速減輕症狀，再改為每次 1.8–2 Gy 完成根治療程；若純屬緩和治療，可持續使用較大單次劑量。

整體約 60–70% 病人有良好至極佳反應，約 20% 在 2 週內呼吸困難、水腫與靜脈擴張完全消失。Rowell 與 Gleeson 統合分析顯示：SCLC 症狀緩解率為化療 77%、放療 78%；NSCLC 為化療 59%、放療 63%。Washington University 回溯研究顯示，接受放療病人中 80% 有良好至極佳症狀緩解；2 週緩解率為起始 3–4 Gy／次 ×3 次者 70%，每次 2 Gy 者 56%。

高頻考點：SVC syndrome 不一定是急症；真正急症是氣道、神經學或血流動力學受損。先穩定病人；不穩定者先置放支架，穩定者先取得組織診斷，再依病理選擇化學治療、放射治療或手術。

Part II. Superior vena cava syndrome

1. Pathophysiology

Superior vena cava syndrome results from extrinsic compression or intrinsic obstruction of the superior vena cava, impairing venous return from the head, neck, upper limbs, and upper torso and causing proximal venous congestion. The SVC carries approximately one third of total venous return, draining the head, arms, and upper torso. The right and left brachiocephalic veins join to form the SVC, which enters the right atrium from the sternal angle. Collateral routes include the azygos, mammary, vertebral, lateral thoracic, paraspinous, and esophageal veins.

2. Presentation

Typical symptoms and signs include facial edema, arm edema, distended neck veins, distended chest veins, facial plethora, dyspnea, cough, hoarseness, stridor, syncope, headache, dizziness, confusion, and obtundation. In the teaching table, the most common findings are facial edema, distended neck veins, dyspnea or cough, distended chest veins, and arm edema. Stridor, confusion, and obtundation are less common but indicate possible life-threatening airway or neurologic compromise.

3. Common causes

Historically, approximately 90% of SVC syndrome was caused by malignancy. With the increased use of pacemaker leads and central venous catheters, malignancy now accounts for about 60%. Nonmalignant causes include catheter-induced thrombosis, chronic mediastinitis, retrosternal goiter, congestive heart failure, and aortic aneurysm. Among malignant causes, NSCLC is most common, followed by SCLC. SCLC can cause more acute presentations because of rapid central airway growth. NHL is an important cause, especially in patients younger than 50 years, with histologies including DLBCL, lymphoblastic lymphoma, and primary mediastinal B-cell lymphoma. Other causes include metastases, germ-cell tumor, thymoma, and mesothelioma.

4. Workup

The workup starts with stability: symptom duration and severity, respiratory status, neurologic status, and hemodynamic stability. History and physical examination should assess prior cancer diagnosis and prior intravascular procedures, and facial changes can be compared with a driver’s license photo or family report. Imaging includes chest radiography for mediastinal widening and contrast-enhanced chest CT for obstruction and collateral vessels. Laboratory tests include BMP and CBC; if cancer is unknown, obtain LDH, AFP, and β-hCG. Externally compressive lesions require tissue diagnosis, using bronchoscopy/EBUS, mediastinoscopy, thoracentesis, or VATS as appropriate.

6. Treatment

Initial management includes head-of-bed elevation, supplemental oxygen, and dexamethasone or diuretics when cerebral edema is present. If thrombosis is present, give anticoagulation. If the patient has respiratory, neurologic, or hemodynamic compromise, endovascular stenting is first-line because it provides the fastest symptomatic relief. If the situation is not immediately life-threatening, obtain pathology before cancer-directed treatment, because radiotherapy and steroids can affect histologic diagnosis. Cancer-directed therapy depends on histology: lymphoma, SCLC, and germ-cell tumors often respond rapidly to chemotherapy; SCLC and NSCLC can be treated with radiotherapy; thymoma may be surgical.

7. Radiotherapy for SVC syndrome

Radiotherapy can improve SVC syndrome within 72 hours, especially for radiosensitive histologies. If the intent remains curative, a short initial course of 3–4 Gy per fraction for 2–3 days can be used for rapid symptom relief, followed by 1.8–2 Gy fractions to complete definitive treatment. If the intent is purely palliative, larger fractions can be continued. Overall, about 60–70% have good-to-excellent responses, and about 20% have complete resolution of dyspnea, edema, and venous distention within 2 weeks. Rowell and Gleeson reported similar relief with chemotherapy and radiotherapy in SCLC and NSCLC. A Washington University retrospective series found good-to-excellent symptomatic relief in 80% of patients receiving radiotherapy, with faster two-week relief using initial 3–4 Gy fractions compared with 2 Gy fractions.

SVC high-yield: SVC syndrome is not always an emergency. The true emergencies are airway, neurologic, or hemodynamic compromise. Stabilize first; if compromised, stent first. If stable, obtain tissue diagnosis and then choose chemotherapy, radiotherapy, or surgery according to histology.

出血、氣道阻塞、咳血與 QUADSHOT

第三部分：出血、氣道阻塞與咳血

一、氣道阻塞與咳血

鄰近或侵犯氣道的腫瘤可造成壓迫、阻塞、出血或咳血。若病人有明顯急性氣道受損，不應先做放射治療，因為放療通常不是恢復氣道最快的方法。急性氣道受損或大量咳血應先接受支氣管鏡、支架、雷射或局部止血處置；病人穩定後，放射治療可提供較持久的症狀緩解。

咳血通常在24–48 小時開始緩解；呼吸困難可能需要約 1 週。

二、ASTRO 非小細胞肺癌緩和性胸腔放射治療

ASTRO 2011 指引指出，體能狀態良好的病人可使用較高劑量的緩和性體外放射治療（EBRT），例如30 Gy／10 次或以上的等效劑量，可能帶來輕度整體存活獲益，但食道毒性較高。體能狀態差者適合短療程，例如20 Gy／5 次、17 Gy／2 次或 10 Gy／1 次，症狀緩解佳且副作用較少。

2018 更新指出，第 III 期 NSCLC 若不適合根治治療，但 ECOG 0–2、可接受化學治療且預期壽命 ≥3 個月，可考慮含鉑雙藥化療合併中度低分割緩和性胸腔放射治療，常用 20 Gy／5 次、30 Gy／10 次或 42 Gy／15 次。第 IV 期 NSCLC 則不建議在臨床試驗或登錄研究外常規使用同步放化療，因缺乏第一級證據。

三、消化道與骨盆出血

放射治療對止血非常有效。肺部出血在放療結束時與追蹤時的改善率約為 100%／80%；上消化道出血為 100%／60%；子宮陰道出血為 95%／79%；膀胱出血為 65%／59%。

Tey 等人 2007 回溯研究納入 33 位局部晚期或復發性胃癌病人，多數接受 30 Gy／10 次，劑量範圍為 8 Gy／1 次至 40 Gy／16 次。出血反應率 54.3%，中位持續 140 天；阻塞與疼痛反應率皆為 25%。僅 1 例發生第 3 級噁心／嘔吐並需住院補液與止吐，沒有治療相關死亡。

四、骨盆出血 QUADSHOT：RTOG 8502

骨盆 QUADSHOT 使用3.7 Gy ×4 次、每日兩次、連續 2 天，每療程 14.8 Gy，可每 4 週重複，最多 3 個療程，總劑量 44.4 Gy。

RTOG 8502 第二期試驗納入 142 位骨盆惡性腫瘤緩和治療病人；60% 完成 1 個療程、20% 完成 2 個療程、20% 完成 3 個療程。整體完全緩解（CR）10%、部分緩解（PR）22%；完成 3 個療程者 CR 14%、PR 31%。毒性低，僅各 1 例急性與晚期腸胃道毒性。

五、頭頸部出血 QUADSHOT

頭頸部 QUADSHOT 使用3.5 Gy ×4 次、每日兩次、連續 2 天，每療程 14 Gy，可每 4 週重複，最多 3 個療程，總劑量 42 Gy。

Corry 等人 2005 第二期研究納入 35 位頭頸部鱗狀細胞癌病人，最常見症狀為吞嚥困難與疼痛。整體 CR／PR 為 53%，77% 有腫瘤縮小或穩定，且沒有第 3 級以上毒性。

高頻考點：急性氣道受損先做支氣管鏡／支架／雷射，穩定後再放療。止血是緩和性放射治療的重要適應症；消化道、骨盆或頭頸部出血且病人體能狀態差、需要快速短療程時，可考慮 QUADSHOT。

Part III. Bleeding, airway obstruction, and hemoptysis

1. Airway obstruction and hemoptysis

Tumor adjacent to or invading the airway can cause compression, obstruction, bleeding, or hemoptysis. If the patient has significant acute airway compromise, radiotherapy should not be the first maneuver because it is usually not the fastest way to restore airway stability. Acute airway compromise or massive hemoptysis should first prompt bronchoscopy, stenting, laser therapy, and local stabilization of the bleeding source. Radiotherapy can then provide more durable palliation once the patient is stable.

Hemoptysis palliation often takes 24–48 hours; dyspnea may take up to one week to improve.

2. ASTRO palliative thoracic radiotherapy for NSCLC

The 2011 ASTRO guideline states that patients with good performance status may benefit from higher-dose palliative thoracic EBRT, such as 30 Gy in 10 fractions or an equivalent or greater dose, with a possible modest survival benefit but higher esophageal toxicity. Patients with poor performance status can receive shorter courses such as 20 Gy in 5 fractions, 17 Gy in 2 fractions, or 10 Gy in 1 fraction, with good symptom relief and fewer side effects.

The 2018 update notes that selected stage III NSCLC patients who are not candidates for curative therapy but have ECOG 0–2, can tolerate chemotherapy, and have life expectancy of at least 3 months may be considered for platinum-containing doublet chemotherapy with moderately hypofractionated palliative thoracic radiotherapy, such as 20/5, 30/10, or 42/15. For stage IV NSCLC, routine concurrent chemoradiation outside a clinical trial or registry is not recommended because level I evidence is lacking.

3. Gastrointestinal and pelvic bleeding

Radiotherapy is highly effective for bleeding control. The lecture table reports improvement at the end of radiotherapy and at follow-up for lung bleeding, upper gastrointestinal bleeding, uterovaginal bleeding, and bladder bleeding.

For gastric bleeding, Tey et al. 2007 retrospectively reviewed 33 patients with locally advanced or recurrent gastric cancer treated with palliative radiotherapy alone, most commonly 30 Gy in 10 fractions. The bleeding response was 54.3%, with median duration of 140 days. Obstruction and pain responses were lower. Only one patient had grade 3 nausea or vomiting requiring hospitalization for intravenous hydration and antiemetics, and there was no treatment-related fatality.

4. QUADSHOT for pelvic bleeding: RTOG 8502

Pelvic QUADSHOT uses 3.7 Gy ×4 fractions, delivered twice daily over 2 consecutive days, for 14.8 Gy per course. It can be repeated every 4 weeks for up to 3 courses, to a total dose of 44.4 Gy. RTOG 8502 enrolled 142 patients receiving pelvic malignancy palliation. Overall complete remission was 10% and partial remission 22%; after 3 courses, complete remission was 14% and partial remission 31%. Toxicity was low, with only one acute gastrointestinal toxicity and one late gastrointestinal toxicity.

5. QUADSHOT for head and neck bleeding

Head and neck QUADSHOT uses 3.5 Gy ×4 fractions, delivered twice daily over 2 consecutive days, for 14 Gy per course. It can be repeated every 4 weeks for up to 3 courses, to a total dose of 42 Gy. Corry et al. 2005 enrolled 35 patients with head and neck squamous cell carcinoma; the most common symptoms were dysphagia and pain. Overall CR/PR was 53%, and 77% had disease reduction or stabilization. Treatment was well tolerated, with no grade 3 or higher toxicity.

Bleeding high-yield: Airway compromise comes first: bronchoscopy, stent, or laser before radiotherapy. Once stable, radiotherapy is useful. Bleeding control is one of the strongest indications for palliative radiotherapy; for GI, pelvic, or head and neck bleeding in frail patients or when rapid short-course palliation is needed, QUADSHOT is a useful option.

骨轉移：評估、預後、骨折風險、術後放射治療、劑量與放射性藥物

第四部分：骨轉移

一、流行病學

有症狀骨轉移是緩和性放射治療最常見的適應症之一。最多可有 80% 癌症病人發生骨轉移，通常經血行播散，也可能由鄰近腫瘤直接侵犯。骨轉移最常侵犯中軸骨骼，包括頭顱、脊椎、胸骨與肋骨；脊椎以胸椎最常受侵犯。常見原發癌包括攝護腺癌、乳癌、腎細胞癌、甲狀腺癌與肺癌。

骨病灶可分為溶骨性、成骨性與混合型。溶骨性病灶在 CT 上呈低密度，接近軟組織密度；成骨性病灶呈高密度。常見溶骨性原發癌包括多發性骨髓瘤、腎細胞癌、甲狀腺癌、黑色素瘤、淋巴瘤、子宮癌、消化道腫瘤、肝細胞癌、鱗狀細胞癌與肉瘤；成骨性病灶最典型為攝護腺癌，也可見於骨肉瘤與軟骨肉瘤；乳癌、肺癌、子宮頸癌與睪丸癌可呈混合型。

二、檢查評估

完整病史與理學檢查應評估疼痛、感覺與運動功能、行走能力、即將骨折風險，以及是否侵犯承重骨。影像可先做 X 光，但 CT 敏感度較高；懷疑脊髓壓迫時需做 MRI；乳癌與攝護腺癌可使用骨掃描。

需要組織診斷的情境包括單發骨腫瘤、沒有既知癌症診斷的新骨病灶，以及首次轉移性復發；優先採 CT 導引細針抽吸或粗針切片。承重骨或骨折風險高者應會診骨科；懷疑脊髓壓迫或顱骨轉移者應會診神經外科。

三、預後評估

Chow 等人 2008 預後模型的三項不良因子為：非乳癌原發、存在骨骼以外轉移，以及 Karnofsky 體能狀態（KPS）≤60%。0–1 項不良因子的中位存活為 60 週，2 項為 25 週，3 項為 10 週。

Kubota／Katagiri 類型模型依原發癌生長速度、內臟轉移、實驗室資料、ECOG 體能狀態、既往化療與多發骨轉移分類。生長較慢者包括乳癌、攝護腺癌、甲狀腺癌、多發性骨髓瘤與淋巴瘤；中等者包括腎癌、子宮內膜癌、卵巢癌與肉瘤；快速者包括肺癌、大腸直腸癌、胃癌、胰臟癌、頭頸癌、食道癌、黑色素瘤、膽囊癌、子宮頸癌及其他泌尿道癌。

四、複雜與非複雜骨轉移

複雜與非複雜骨轉移
分類定義

複雜骨轉移存在病理性骨折、脊髓／馬尾壓迫；臨床上也常納入明顯軟組織腫塊或承重骨高骨折風險

五、骨折風險與預防性固定

Harrington 預防性固定準則包括：皮質骨破壞 >50%、幹骺端侵犯 >2.5 cm、股骨轉子下病灶破壞，以及放療後持續疼痛。此準則早於 Mirels，但驗證不足，使用仍有爭議。

Mirels 評分依長骨病灶的位置、大小、性質與疼痛預測病理性骨折風險；其中疼痛最具主觀性，因此可能稍微高估骨折風險。

六、手術與術後放射治療

手術選項包括椎體成形術、椎體後凸成形術與髓內釘固定。椎體成形術是經皮將聚甲基丙烯酸甲酯（PMMA）骨水泥注入塌陷椎體；椎體後凸成形術先以氣球在骨折椎體內形成空腔，再注入骨水泥；髓內釘則用於長骨穩定，可合併骨水泥。

Townsend 等人 1995 顯示，骨科固定後的術後放射治療（PORT）非常重要。PORT 通常於術後 6 週內開始，中位劑量 30 Gy；正常使用肢體且疼痛可有可無的比例為PORT 53%，單獨手術 11.5%，且可降低再次手術需求。

術後放射治療應盡量涵蓋整段固定器材。MSKCC 回溯研究顯示，完整涵蓋器材與較低局部復發相關；多發性骨髓瘤長骨固定後，若器材涵蓋 <80%，器材周圍復發風險增加。

七、非複雜骨轉移的放射治療劑量

RTOG 7402 顯示多種分割皆可緩解疼痛，約 90% 有部分緩解，54% 完全緩解。Rich 等人 2018 納入 29 項隨機試驗的統合分析顯示，單次與多次分割在疼痛緩解與毒性上相近：整體反應率 61% 對 62%，完全反應 23% 對 24%；病理性骨折、脊髓壓迫與急性毒性無顯著差異，但再次照射率為單次 20%，多次 8%。

RTOG 9714 比較乳癌／攝護腺癌疼痛性骨轉移的8 Gy／1 次與 30 Gy／10 次。整體反應率幾乎相同：65% 對 66%；麻醉性止痛藥減量 33% 對 32%；骨折 5% 對 4%；晚期第 2 級以上毒性 4% 對 4%。8 Gy／1 次急性毒性較低，但再次照射較高：18% 對 9%。

ASTRO 2017 更新指出，未曾照射的骨轉移使用8 Gy／1 次、24 Gy／6 次或 30 Gy／10 次，疼痛緩解效果相等。單次治療的再次照射率較高，但不增加病理性骨折或晚期毒性。持續或復發疼痛可於 EBRT 後 1 個月考慮再次照射，並遵守正常組織限制。手術、放射性核種、雙磷酸鹽、椎體後凸成形術與椎體成形術均不能取代 EBRT。

八、高風險無症狀骨轉移

放射治療並非只用於有症狀病灶。Gillespie 等人 2022 第二期隨機試驗比較高風險無症狀骨轉移接受預防性放療或標準照護。高風險定義包括腫瘤 ≥2 cm、脊椎交界區或後方骨性結構病灶、髖關節／薦髂關節侵犯，以及長骨病灶侵犯三分之一至三分之二皮質厚度。

骨骼相關事件（SRE）為標準照護組 29%，預防性放療組 1.6%；預防性放療亦改善至首次 SRE 時間、住院、疼痛與整體存活。

九、再次照射

RTOG 0433 比較疼痛性骨轉移再次照射使用 8 Gy／1 次與 20 Gy／5–8 次；2 個月疼痛反應率無差異，單次治療的食慾下降、嘔吐、腹瀉與皮膚紅斑較少。

十、疼痛驟升與 dexamethasone

疼痛驟升是治療後轉移骨病灶短暫疼痛惡化。EBRT 後發生率可達 44%，SBRT 後可達 64%，會降低生活品質並增加止痛藥副作用。Dexamethasone 可減少治療區骨膜水腫，但最佳劑量、頻率與療程仍無共識。Dutch DEXA 研究顯示 dexamethasone 可立即減輕疼痛，但未明確降低疼痛驟升發生率，可能只是延後發生。

十一、多發性骨髓瘤骨病灶放射治療

多發性骨髓瘤常可使用較低生物有效劑量。疼痛性骨漿細胞瘤常用 8 Gy／1 次、20 Gy／5 次或 20–30 Gy／10–20 次；30 Gy／10–15 次保留給大型腫塊、脊髓壓迫或需要持久控制者。回溯資料顯示最常使用 20 Gy／10 次，脊椎是最常照射部位；BED10 <28 Gy 的再次照射率稍高，但整體再次照射率仍低。

十二、骨骼修飾藥物與放射性藥物

Pamidronate 與 zoledronate 為雙磷酸鹽，可抑制破骨細胞，風險包括顎骨壞死與非典型股骨骨折；denosumab 為 RANKL 抑制劑，可阻止破骨細胞成熟。

非密封放射源包括 Sr-89、Sm-153、P-32 與 Ra-223。P-32 因血液毒性高而較少使用。Sr-89 為純 β 發射核種，半衰期 50.6 天；Sm-153 主要發射 β 粒子並具有 γ 成分，半衰期 46 小時、由尿液快速排除，疼痛緩解較快；Ra-223 為 α 發射核種，半衰期 11.4 天。

Ra-223 適用於轉移性去勢抗性攝護腺癌，且病人無明顯骨髓抑制、腎功能可接受、無脊髓壓迫或即將骨折、無明顯軟組織成分，並且不與 abiraterone 併用。ALSYMPCA：OS 14.9 對 11.3 個月；首次症狀性骨骼事件 15.6 對 9.8 個月。Sr-89／Sm-153 的骨髓毒性較高，適合血球計數可接受且具有多發疼痛性骨轉移的病人。

高頻考點：8 Gy／1 次與 30 Gy／10 次的疼痛緩解相近；8 Gy／1 次方便、急性毒性較低，但再次照射較高。預後較佳、術後、複雜病灶或需要持久控制者可採較長療程；術後應盡量完整涵蓋固定器材。

Part IV. Bone metastases

1. Epidemiology

Symptomatic bone metastases are among the most common indications for palliative radiotherapy. Bone metastases can occur in up to 80% of cancer patients, usually through hematogenous spread, although direct invasion can occur. They most often involve the axial skeleton, including skull, spine, sternum, and ribs; within the spine, the thoracic spine is most commonly affected. The five most common primaries are prostate, breast, renal cell carcinoma, thyroid, and lung cancer.

Bone lesions can be lytic, sclerotic, or mixed. Lytic lesions are usually lucent on CT and approach soft-tissue density, whereas sclerotic lesions are hyperdense. Osteolytic primaries include multiple myeloma, RCC, thyroid, melanoma, lymphoma, uterine, GI malignancies, HCC, squamous cell carcinoma, and sarcoma. Osteoblastic metastases are classically prostate cancer. Breast, lung, cervical, and testicular cancers can be mixed.

2. Workup

History and physical examination should assess pain, sensory or motor dysfunction, walking ability, impending fracture risk, and whether a weight-bearing bone is involved. Imaging can start with radiography, but CT is more sensitive. If spinal cord compression is a concern, obtain MRI. Breast and prostate cancer may be evaluated with bone scintigraphy. Tissue diagnosis is needed for a solitary bone tumor, a new bone tumor without a known cancer diagnosis, or first metastatic relapse; CT-guided FNA or core biopsy is preferred.

Consult orthopedics for weight-bearing bone lesions or fracture concern, and neurosurgery for cord compression or skull metastasis concern.

3. Prognostication

The Chow et al. JCO 2008 model uses three adverse factors: non-breast primary, non-bony metastases, and KPS ≤60%. Median survival was 60 weeks for 0–1 risk factor, 25 weeks for 2 factors, and 10 weeks for 3 factors. Kubota/Katagiri-type frameworks use primary cancer speed, visceral metastases, laboratory data, ECOG performance status, prior chemotherapy, and multiple skeletal metastases. Slow primaries include breast, prostate, thyroid, multiple myeloma, and lymphoma; moderate primaries include renal, endometrial, ovarian, and sarcoma; rapid primaries include lung, colorectal, gastric, pancreatic, head and neck, esophageal, melanoma, gallbladder, cervical, and other urologic cancers.

4. Complicated versus uncomplicated bone metastases

UncomplicatedPainful bone metastases without impending or existing pathologic fracture and without spinal cord or cauda equina compression.

This classification affects fractionation. Uncomplicated painful bone metastases can be treated confidently with 8 Gy ×1. Complicated lesions often require surgical assessment, longer-course radiotherapy, or more precise technique.

5. Fracture risk and prophylactic fixation

Harrington criteria for prophylactic fixation include cortical bone destruction greater than 50%, metaphyseal involvement greater than 2.5 cm, destruction of a subtrochanteric femoral lesion, and persistent pain after radiotherapy. These criteria predate Mirels and have not been fully validated. Mirels score uses long-bone lesion site, size, nature, and pain to predict pathologic fracture risk; pain is the most subjective component, so Mirels may somewhat overestimate fracture risk.

6. Surgery and postoperative radiotherapy

Surgical options include vertebroplasty, kyphoplasty, and intramedullary rod fixation. Vertebroplasty injects PMMA bone cement into a collapsed vertebral body. Kyphoplasty first uses a balloon to create a cavity in the fractured vertebral body before cement injection. Intramedullary rods are used for long-bone stabilization, often with cementing.

Townsend et al. 1995 showed that postoperative radiotherapy after orthopedic stabilization is important. Postoperative radiotherapy was the only multivariable factor significantly associated with good functional status; it was typically started within 6 weeks after surgery, with median dose 30 Gy. Postoperative radiotherapy improved normal use with or without pain and reduced the need for second surgery. Whole-hardware coverage is also important: MSKCC retrospective data showed lower local recurrence with whole-hardware coverage after propensity-score matching, and in multiple myeloma, less than 80% hardware coverage increased hardware relapse risk.

7. Radiotherapy dose for uncomplicated bone metastases

RTOG 7402 showed that multiple fractionation schedules provide pain relief. Rich et al. 2018 meta-analysis of 29 randomized trials found similar pain relief and toxicity between single-fraction and multifraction palliative radiotherapy for uncomplicated bone metastases. Overall response, complete response, pathologic fracture, cord compression, and acute toxicity were similar, but retreatment was more common after single-fraction treatment.

RTOG 9714 compared 8 Gy ×1 with 30 Gy in 10 fractions for painful breast or prostate bone metastases. Overall response was essentially identical, narcotic relief and fracture rates were similar, and late grade 2 or higher toxicity was similar. The 8 Gy arm had less acute grade 2 or higher toxicity but more retreatment.

The ASTRO 2017 update states that for previously unirradiated bone metastases, 8 Gy in 1 fraction, 24 Gy in 6 fractions, and 30 Gy in 10 fractions provide equivalent pain relief. Single-fraction treatment has higher retreatment rates but does not increase pathologic fracture or late toxicity. Persistent or recurrent pain can be retreated one month after EBRT, while respecting normal tissue constraints. Surgery, radionuclides, bisphosphonates, kyphoplasty, and vertebroplasty do not replace EBRT.

8. High-risk asymptomatic bone metastases

Radiotherapy is not only for symptomatic lesions. Gillespie et al. 2022 phase II randomized trial compared prophylactic radiotherapy with standard care for high-risk asymptomatic bone metastases. High-risk features included bulky disease ≥2 cm, junctional spine or posterior spinal element disease, hip or sacroiliac joint involvement, and long-bone lesions involving one third to two thirds of cortical thickness. Skeletal-related events were 29% with standard care versus 1.6% with prophylactic radiotherapy; prophylactic radiotherapy also improved time to SRE, hospitalizations, pain, and survival.

9. Re-irradiation

RTOG 0433 compared 8 Gy in 1 fraction with 20 Gy in 5–8 fractions for reirradiation of painful bone metastases. The primary endpoint was 2-month pain relief. Overall response was similar, while single-fraction treatment had less toxicity, including appetite loss, vomiting, diarrhea, and skin redness.

10. Pain flare and dexamethasone

Pain flare is transient worsening pain at the treated metastatic bone site. It can occur after EBRT and is even more frequent after SBRT, affecting quality of life and increasing analgesic side effects. Dexamethasone has been studied for prophylaxis, possibly by reducing periosteal edema in treated bone, but optimal dose, frequency, and duration are not established. In the Dutch DEXA study, dexamethasone had an immediate effect on pain, but prophylaxis did not clearly reduce pain flare incidence and may have only postponed it.

11. Multiple myeloma bone radiotherapy

Multiple myeloma often requires lower BED. Common regimens include 8 Gy in 1 fraction, 20 Gy in 5 fractions, and 20–30 Gy in 10–20 fractions for painful osseous plasmacytomas. Thirty Gy in 10–15 fractions is reserved for bulky masses or spinal cord compression when durable control is needed. Retrospective data suggest that 20 Gy in 10 fractions is common, especially for spine treatment; BED10 below 28 Gy slightly increases reirradiation but overall reirradiation remains low.

12. Bone-modifying agents and radiopharmaceuticals

Bone-modifying agents include pamidronate and zoledronate, bisphosphonates that inhibit osteoclasts, with risks including osteonecrosis of the jaw and atypical femur fracture. Denosumab is a RANKL inhibitor that blocks osteoclast maturation.

Unsealed sources include Sr-89, Sm-153, P-32, and Ra-223. P-32 is rarely used because of hematologic toxicity. Sr-89 is a pure beta emitter with a 50.6-day half-life. Sm-153 emits beta radiation with a gamma component, has a 46-hour half-life, is cleared rapidly in urine, and provides relatively rapid pain relief. Ra-223 is an alpha emitter with an 11.4-day half-life. Ra-223 is appropriate for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer without myelosuppression, with adequate kidney function, no cord compression or impending fracture, no soft-tissue component, and not combined with abiraterone. ALSYMPCA showed an approximately 3-month survival benefit and delayed first symptomatic skeletal event. Sr-89 and Sm-153 have more marrow toxicity and are most suitable for patients with acceptable blood counts and multifocal painful metastases.

Bone metastases high-yield: 8 Gy ×1 and 30 Gy in 10 fractions provide similar pain relief. 8 Gy ×1 is convenient and has lower acute toxicity but higher retreatment. Longer courses are used for better prognosis, postoperative settings, complicated lesions, or need for durable control. Cover the entire hardware whenever possible.

轉移性脊髓壓迫：解剖、臨床表現、評估、SINS 與 Bilsky 分級

第五部分：轉移性脊髓壓迫的基礎評估

一、解剖與定義

成人脊髓由枕骨大孔延伸至 L1–L2；馬尾為 L1–L2 至 S2 的腰薦神經根；硬膜囊包覆脊髓，神經根由外側離開。MRI 上腦脊髓液（CSF）在 T2 呈高訊號、T1 呈低訊號。T1 異常低訊號常表示正常脂肪骨髓消失與含水量增加；T2 異常高訊號可見於創傷、感染或腫瘤。

脊髓壓迫是影像上任何脊髓或馬尾受到壓迫的狀態。可分為硬膜外壓迫，通常由腫瘤或骨片造成；硬膜內病灶；硬膜內髓外病灶，例如軟腦膜疾病；以及髓內轉移。

二、發生率與臨床表現

轉移性脊髓壓迫（MSCC）影響約5–10% 癌症病人，是具有高失能與死亡風險的腫瘤急症。中位存活約 3 個月，壓迫後僅約三分之一病人存活超過 1 年。成人常見原發癌依序為肺癌、攝護腺癌與多發性骨髓瘤；兒童常見尤文肉瘤與神經母細胞瘤。

約 95% 為硬膜外壓迫，通常由椎體轉移外壓造成；胸椎約 70%、腰椎 20%、頸椎 10%；約 25% 病人同時有多個節段受侵犯。

最常見症狀是背痛，發生率 83–95%。疼痛可為局部或神經根性，常於平躺或 Valsalva 動作時惡化，夜間或清晨較明顯。癌症病人出現新發背痛必須高度警覺，因背痛常比神經症狀早數週出現。

其他症狀包括無力 60–80%、不能行走 48–77%、感覺異常約 50%，可呈帶狀、上升型或鞍區麻木；自主神經功能障礙 40–64%，包括大小便功能障礙與尿滯留。

三、病理生理

若壓迫逐漸進展，可能先造成硬膜外靜脈鬱血、血管性水腫與脫髓鞘，此階段可能可逆，且緩慢進展時可建立側枝循環。若快速進展，可能造成動脈阻塞、缺血與梗塞，較不可逆。

脊髓症候群包括完全橫斷症候群、前脊髓症候群與後脊髓症候群。完全橫斷造成病灶以下所有感覺喪失、無力與大小便功能障礙；前脊髓症候群造成無力與痛溫覺喪失；後脊髓症候群造成本體覺與震動覺喪失、無力及共濟失調。

四、檢查評估

需進行聚焦中樞神經與肌肉骨骼檢查，記錄症狀發生時間、癌症病史、轉移範圍、既往放療、疼痛與 ECOG／KPS。影像標準為有無 gadolinium 顯影的全脊椎 MRI，敏感度約 93%、特異度約 97%。應涵蓋整個神經軸，因可能同時存在多發且無症狀病灶。

CT 可評估骨性結構與疾病範圍。若無法接受 MRI，例如金屬植入物或特定心律調節器，可使用 CT 脊髓攝影，其診斷效能相近。

五、脊椎腫瘤不穩定性評分

脊椎腫瘤不穩定性評分（SINS）評估腫瘤相關脊椎不穩定性，協助決定活動限制與手術處置。評分項目包括位置、機械性疼痛、骨病灶類型、脊椎排列、椎體塌陷與後外側結構侵犯。

SINS 0–6 為穩定；7–12 為可能不穩定；13–18 為不穩定。一般 SINS ≥7 即應考慮神經外科或脊椎外科會診。

Part V. Cord compression / metastatic spinal cord compression

1. Anatomy and definition

In adults, the spinal cord extends from the foramen magnum to L1–L2. The cauda equina consists of lumbar and sacral nerve roots from L1–L2 to S2. The dural sac surrounds the spinal cord, and nerve roots exit laterally. On MRI, CSF is bright on T2 and dark on T1. Abnormal low T1 signal often indicates loss of normal fatty marrow and increased water content; abnormal high T2 signal can reflect trauma, infection, cancer, or other pathology.

Cord compression is any radiographic compression of the spinal cord or cauda equina. It can be epidural compression from tumor or bone fragment, intradural disease, intradural extramedullary disease such as leptomeningeal disease, or intramedullary tumor deposit within the spinal cord.

2. Incidence and presentation

MSCC affects approximately 5–10% of cancer patients and is an oncologic emergency with high morbidity and mortality. Median survival is about 3 months, and only about one third of patients live longer than one year after cord compression. In adults, the most common causes are lung cancer, prostate cancer, and multiple myeloma. In children, Ewing sarcoma and neuroblastoma are common. Most cases are epidural, usually due to vertebral body metastasis causing external compression. The thoracic spine is most common, followed by lumbar and cervical spine, and about one quarter of patients have multiple levels involved.

The most common presenting symptom is back pain, occurring in 83–95%. Pain may be localized or radicular, often worsens with recumbency or Valsalva, and is often worse at night or early morning. New back pain in a cancer patient must be taken seriously because back pain often precedes neurologic symptoms by weeks. Other symptoms include weakness, non-ambulatory status, sensory deficits such as band-like, ascending, or saddle anesthesia, and autonomic dysfunction including bowel or bladder dysfunction and urinary retention.

3. Pathophysiology

With gradual progression, MSCC may first cause epidural venous congestion, vasogenic edema, and demyelination, which may be reversible. Slow progression may allow collaterals to develop. Rapid progression can cause arterial occlusion, ischemia, and infarction, which is less reversible. Spinal cord syndromes include cord transection, ventral cord syndrome, and dorsal cord syndrome. Transection causes loss of all sensory modalities below the lesion, weakness, and bowel/bladder dysfunction. Ventral cord syndrome causes weakness and loss of pain and temperature. Dorsal cord syndrome causes loss of proprioception and vibration, weakness, and ataxia.

4. Workup

Workup requires focused CNS and musculoskeletal examination, documenting symptom onset, cancer history, extent of metastatic disease, prior radiotherapy, pain, and ECOG/KPS. The imaging gold standard is whole-spine MRI with and without gadolinium, with high sensitivity and specificity. Image the entire neuraxis because synchronous multifocal asymptomatic sites can occur. CT is useful for osseous anatomy and disease extent. If MRI cannot be performed, for example because of metal or pacemaker, CT myelogram can be used with similar diagnostic performance.

5. SINS score

SINS evaluates neoplastic spinal instability and helps guide mobilization and operative management. Criteria include location, mechanical pain, bone lesion type, spinal alignment, vertebral body collapse, and posterolateral involvement. A total score of 0–6 is stable, 7–12 is potentially unstable and needs further evaluation, and 13–18 is unstable and strongly predicts need for surgical stabilization. In general, score ≥7 should prompt neurosurgical consultation.

七、立即處置：先給類固醇

懷疑 MSCC 時，第一步是立即給予類固醇，作為根治性處置前的橋接治療。作用機轉是降低發炎與脊髓水腫，且對多發性骨髓瘤與淋巴瘤可能具有直接腫瘤溶解作用。需監測高血糖與高血壓，並使用質子幫浦抑制劑或 H2 阻斷劑預防腸胃道副作用。

高劑量 dexamethasone 的毒性不可忽視。Vecht 1989 比較 100 mg 與 10 mg 靜脈負荷劑量，再接每日口服 16 mg，疼痛、行走與膀胱功能無差異。Heimdal 1992 使用每日 96 mg 高劑量時，出現嚴重腸胃道出血、穿孔、肺炎、傷口感染與持續高血糖，支持較低劑量已足夠。

典型處方為dexamethasone 10 mg 靜脈負荷，之後 4–6 mg 每 6–8 小時一次；24–48 小時後可改口服，放療完成後逐步減量，例如每 4 天將總劑量減半，並給予腸胃道保護。

八、預後評估：Rades

Rades 2002 顯示，運動缺損形成速度是功能恢復的重要預後因子。若運動缺損在放療前 1–7 天內形成，放療後可行走率 35%、症狀改善率 10%；8–14 天為 55%／29%；>14 天為86%／86%。快速惡化較可能代表不可逆脊髓梗塞；緩慢缺血則可能因側枝循環形成而較可逆。

Rades 2008 評分可預測放療後行走能力與存活。2011 驗證分為三組：≤28 分可採短療程緩解疼痛；29–37 分可考慮椎板切除／穩定手術加放療；≥38 分使用單獨放療也可能有良好效果。高分組 6 個月整體存活可接近 80–99%。

九、放射治療與手術的選擇

放射治療耐受度佳、手術風險低，對放射敏感腫瘤特別有效，且可再次照射；缺點是無法穩定脊椎，症狀改善通常需數天。手術可取得組織診斷、立即減壓並穩定脊椎或矯正骨性變形，但年長、虛弱或全身疾病嚴重者手術風險較高。

手術方式包括減壓術、椎板切除術與椎體切除術。減壓術移除腫瘤並達到環周減壓；椎板切除術移除後方椎弓，但可能造成不穩定；椎體切除術由胸腔或後腹腔途徑移除椎體，常需重建與融合，可能使放射治療延後約 6 週。

Patchell 2005 比較直接減壓手術加 30 Gy／10 次放療，與單獨 30 Gy／10 次放療。行走率為84% 對 57%；入組時仍可行走者為 94% 對 74%，行走持續時間 153 對 54 天；入組時不能行走者重新恢復行走為62% 對 19%。手術亦改善失禁持續時間與整體存活。

手術加放療的適合條件通常包括：單一連續區域、體能狀態良好、單一病灶、無內臟或腦轉移、脊椎不穩定、年齡 <65 歲、KPS ≥70%、預期存活 >3 個月、不能行走 <48 小時，以及放射抗性腫瘤。多發性骨髓瘤、生殖細胞腫瘤與 SCLC 等放射敏感腫瘤，可優先考慮單獨放療；多發病灶或不適合手術者亦多採放療。

十二、NOMS 架構

NOMS 代表神經學（Neurologic）、腫瘤學（Oncologic）、機械性（Mechanical）與全身狀態（Systemic）。神經學看 Bilsky 分級；腫瘤學看腫瘤放射敏感度，例如肝細胞癌、腎細胞癌、肉瘤與黑色素瘤較具放射抗性；機械性看 SINS；全身狀態看預後、體能狀態、疾病範圍及能否承受手術或全身治療。

簡化原則：Bilsky 0–1 且脊椎穩定，可偏向 RT／SBRT；Bilsky 2–3、放射抗性腫瘤或 SINS 不穩定，應積極考慮減壓／穩定手術後再放療。

十三、毒性與治療技術

放射性脊髓病變為排除性診斷，臨床可從輕微感覺或運動缺損，進展至完全下身或四肢癱瘓與自主神經功能喪失。總劑量越高、單次劑量越大，潛伏期越短。傳統分割後中位發生時間約 18 個月，再次照射約 11 個月；SBRT 初次治療約 12 個月，再次照射約 6 個月。

QUANTEC 全周脊髓最大劑量與第 2 級放射性脊髓病變風險約為：45 Gy＝0.03%、50 Gy＝0.2%、60 Gy＝6%、69 Gy＝50%。

HYTEC 對 1–5% 放射性脊髓病變風險的 SBRT 點最大劑量約為：1 次 12.4–14 Gy、2 次 17–19.3 Gy、3 次 20.3–23.1 Gy、4 次 23–26.2 Gy、5 次 25.3–28.8 Gy。

7. Immediate management: steroids first

When MSCC is suspected, the first step is steroids as a bridge to definitive treatment. Steroids reduce inflammation and cord edema and may have direct oncolytic effects in multiple myeloma and lymphoma. Monitor hyperglycemia and hypertension, and provide gastrointestinal prophylaxis with a PPI or H2 blocker.

High-dose dexamethasone toxicity is important. Vecht 1989 compared 100 mg versus 10 mg IV loading followed by 16 mg orally daily and found no difference in pain, ambulation, or bladder function. Heimdal 1992 reported severe toxicity with 96 mg IV daily, including GI bleeding, GI perforation, pneumonia, wound infection, and persistent hyperglycemia, supporting lower-dose regimens below 16 mg/day. A typical regimen is dexamethasone 10 mg IV loading, followed by 4–6 mg every 6–8 hours; after 24–48 hours, switch to oral dosing, then taper after radiotherapy, for example halving the total dose every 4 days, with GI prophylaxis.

8. Prognostication: Rades

Rades 2002 showed that the time course of motor deficit development is an important prognostic factor for functional outcome. Among patients with motor deficits treated with radiotherapy, ambulatory rates and symptom improvement were much worse when deficits developed within 1–7 days before radiotherapy compared with slower deterioration over more than 14 days. Rapid deterioration suggests irreversible cord infarction; gradual ischemia may be more reversible because collaterals can develop.

Rades score 2008 predicts post-radiotherapy ambulation and survival. In the 2011 validation, scores ≤28 favored short-course radiotherapy for pain palliation, scores 29–37 could prompt consideration of laminectomy plus stabilization plus radiotherapy, and scores ≥38 had good outcomes with radiotherapy alone. Survival validations similarly separate patients into poor, intermediate, and favorable survival groups.

9. Radiotherapy versus surgery

Radiotherapy is well tolerated, has low morbidity and mortality, is especially effective for radiosensitive tumors, and can be repeated. Its limitations are inability to stabilize the spine and a delay of several days before effect. Surgery provides tissue diagnosis, immediate cord decompression, spine stabilization, and correction of bony deformity, but elderly, debilitated, and systemically ill patients may face surgical morbidity and mortality.

Surgical options include decompression, laminectomy, and corpectomy. Decompression removes tumor and provides immediate circumferential decompression, often through an anterior approach with reconstruction when needed. Laminectomy removes the posterior arch but can destabilize the spine. Corpectomy removes the vertebral body through thoracotomy or retroperitoneal approach and may require delaying radiotherapy for about 6 weeks while fusion occurs; it is best suited for patients with good life expectancy and performance status.

Patchell et al. Lancet 2005 supports direct decompressive surgery plus radiotherapy for selected patients. The trial excluded lymphoma, multiple myeloma, germ-cell tumor, and CNS primaries. All patients received dexamethasone and were randomized to surgery plus 30 Gy in 10 fractions versus radiotherapy alone. Surgery plus radiotherapy improved ambulatory rates, walking duration, recovery of ambulation among non-ambulatory patients, incontinence duration, and survival. Critiques include poor outcomes in the radiotherapy-alone arm, inclusion of unstable spine in that arm, and small sample size.

General indications for surgery plus radiotherapy include a single continuous region, good performance status, solitary site, no visceral or brain metastases, spinal instability, age below 65, KPS ≥70%, anticipated survival over 3 months, non-ambulatory status less than 48 hours, and radioresistant tumor. Radiosensitive tumors such as multiple myeloma, germ-cell tumor, and SCLC may be treated with radiotherapy alone; multiple sites or nonsurgical candidacy also favor radiotherapy alone.

11. Spine SBRT

A Lancet 2021 multicenter randomized phase 2/3 trial enrolled patients with painful MRI-confirmed spinal metastases, up to 3 consecutive vertebral body segments, ECOG 0–2, SINS <12, no prior radiotherapy or surgery, and no neurologically involved symptomatic compression. Patients were randomized to 20 Gy in 5 fractions versus 24 Gy in 2 fractions. SBRT 24 Gy in 2 fractions improved 3-month complete pain response compared with conventional EBRT, without excess severe toxicity or grade 5 events.

12. NOMS framework

NOMS stands for Neurologic, Oncologic, Mechanical, and Systemic. Neurologic assessment mainly uses Bilsky grade. Oncologic assessment evaluates tumor radiosensitivity or radioresistance, with HCC, RCC, sarcoma, and melanoma being more radioresistant. Mechanical assessment uses SINS stability. Systemic assessment considers prognosis, performance status, disease extent, and ability to tolerate surgery or systemic therapy. In simplified form: Bilsky 0–1 and stable spine often favor radiotherapy or SBRT; Bilsky 2–3, radioresistant disease, or unstable SINS should prompt consideration of decompression or stabilization followed by radiotherapy or SBRT.

13. Toxicity and technique

Radiation myelopathy is a diagnosis of exclusion and can range from mild sensory or motor deficits to complete paraplegia or quadriplegia with autonomic loss. Higher total dose and higher dose per fraction shorten latency. Median onset is about 18 months after conventional fractionation, about 11 months after reirradiation, about 12 months after de novo SBRT, and about 6 months after SBRT reirradiation. QUANTEC full-circumference spinal cord constraints and HYTEC SBRT point maximum dose constraints guide safe treatment. For reirradiation SBRT, safety factors include cumulative thecal sac EQD2 Dmax ≤70 Gy, SBRT thecal sac point EQD2 Dmax ≤25 Gy, SBRT/cumulative ratio ≤0.5, and interval at least 5 months.

Palliative spine radiotherapy technique often uses AP/PA or AP/RPO/LPO fields for thoracic, lumbar, or sacral spine. Cervical spine technique depends on anatomy and prior treatment, often using opposed laterals.

One-sentence memory aid: Palliative radiotherapy is not “treat every emergency immediately”; first decide stability, pathology, prior radiotherapy, and whether intervention or surgery is needed. Bone metastases and bleeding can often be relieved effectively with short-course treatment, while MSCC requires immediate steroids and MRI, then SINS/Bilsky/NOMS-based selection of radiotherapy alone, surgery plus radiotherapy, or SBRT.
