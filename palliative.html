🕊️ PALLIATIVE RADIATION

緩和放射治療

骨轉移、脊髓壓迫、SVC 症候群、腫瘤出血的處置。

Bone mets, cord compression, SVC syndrome, and tumor bleeding.

骨轉移

骨轉移

單次 8 Gy vs 30 Gy/10 fx (Chow, RTOG 97-14): 疼痛控制相當；單次再照射率較高但方便。

複雜/脊柱/軟組織: 30 Gy/10 fx or 20 Gy/5 fx。

SBRT 為骨轉移 (RTOG 0631, SC.24/CCTG SC.24 SORCE): 脊椎 24 Gy/2 fx 相對 conventional 20 Gy/5 fx 疼痛完全緩解率更高。

Single 8 Gy vs 30 Gy/10 fx (Chow, RTOG 97-14): Equivalent pain control; single-fx has higher retreatment rate but is convenient.

Complex / spine / soft-tissue extension: 30 Gy/10 fx or 20 Gy/5 fx.

Spine SBRT (RTOG 0631, SC.24): 24 Gy/2 fx improves complete pain response vs conventional 20 Gy/5 fx.

脊髓壓迫

惡性脊髓壓迫

Patchell trial (Lancet 2005): 手術 + RT 相對 RT alone 顯著提升行走與存活。

RT alone dose: 30 Gy/10 fx。
Highly selected: SBRT 為 oligomets 病人的選項。

Patchell trial (Lancet 2005): Surgery + RT significantly improves ambulation and survival vs RT alone.

RT alone dose: 30 Gy/10 fx.
Highly selected: SBRT is an option for oligomets patients.

SVC / 出血

SVC 症候群與出血

SVC: 首選血管內支架快速緩解；後續 RT 依原發癌 (SCLC → 化療優先)。

Hemostatic RT: 婦科 / 泌尿 / 胸腔出血 — 20–30 Gy/5–10 fx 有效止血。

SVC: Endovascular stenting is preferred for rapid relief; subsequent RT depends on primary (SCLC → chemo first).

Hemostatic RT: Gyn / GU / thoracic bleeding — 20–30 Gy/5–10 fx effectively stops bleeding.

關鍵試驗

關鍵臨床試驗

以下為此主題的核心隨機試驗與樞紐研究，資料源自 Estes 2025 Key Studies 資料表，已整合於本章。

Core randomized trials and pivotal studies for this topic, from the Estes 2025 Key Studies tables, are integrated into this chapter.

University of Toronto ⭐️

Bone · Fractionation

Chow et al, Clin Oncol, 2012 Chow et al, Lancet Oncol, 2014 Chow et al, Radiother Oncol, 2019

Population: Bone metastases

Methods: Meta-analysis of 29 trials in most recent publication

Results: ↔️ Equivalent efficacy for ORR, CR, pathologic fracture, and cord compression ✅ Multifraction has lower re-treatment rate •Results stable across meta-analyses Single fraction vs multifraction (meta-analysis of 25 trials) ↔️ ORR 60% single vs 61% mult (NS) ↔️ CR 23% vs 24% (NS) ↔️ No difference in pathologic fractures ⛔ Retreatment 2.6x higher with single fraction, p<0.00001 Overall pain relief with re-tx 45% with 8 Gy/1 and 51% with 20 Gy/5 fx ↔️ No significant differences in acute adverse effects

Interpretation: There is equivalent ORR, CR, pathologic fractions and cord compression efficacy between regimens. Multifraction has lower re-treatment rate. Authors rec no additional funding to be spent on trials.

ROBOMET

Bone · SBRT vs. conventional

Mercier et al, JCO, 2025 · N=126

Population: ≤3 painful bone mets from solid tumor, uncomplicated, pain score ≥2/10

Methods: Phase III ➜ SBRT 20 Gy single fx vs. ➜ EBRT 8 Gy single fx

Results: 1-mo CR 25% vs. 37% 3-mos CR 24% vs. 33% 3-mos CR and treated per protocol 54% vs. 31%

Interpretation: SBRT did not improve CR rates at the primary endpoint of 1 month, but did improve CR rates at 3 months for those treated per protocol.

RTOG 0631

Bone · SBRT vs. conventional

Ryu et al, JAMA Oncol, 2023 · N=353

Population: 1-3 spine metastasis, localized, NRPS score of ≥5

Methods: Phase III ➜ SBRT 16 or 18 Gy single fx (as low as 80% coverage permitted) vs. ➜ EBRT 8 Gy single fx

Results: Pain response seemed better with EBRT, 41% vs. 61%, p=0.99 (one-side p test), and p=0.01 for 2-sided p No difference in adverse effects

Interpretation: Single fraction SBRT and EBRT to spine show no difference in pain response or adverse effects.

CCTG SC.24/ TROG 17.0

Bone · SBRT vs. conventional

Sahgal et al, Lancet Oncol, 2021 Zeng et al, IJROBP, 2022 · N=229

Population: Painful spine metastasis, ≤3 consecutive vertebra (oligo not required)

Methods: Phase III ➜ 🏆 SBRT 24 Gy/ 2 fx vs. ➜ conventional 20 Gy/ 5 fx

Results: ✅ 3-mo pain CR 35% vs. 14% ✅ 2-yr LF 15% vs. 36% Iatrogenic vertebral fractures n=8/12 vs. 4/12 All grade 3 vertebral fractions were with SBRT (n=5/12)

Interpretation: SBRT to 24 Gy in 2 fractions leads to better CR in pain response than conventional RT in 5 fractions.

MDACC

Bone · SBRT vs. multi fraction

Nguyen et al, JAMA Oncol, 2019 · N=160

Population: Bone metastases

Methods: Phase II noinferiority ➜ single fx SBRT vs. ➜ 30 Gy/ 10 fx (12 Gy for ≥4 cm, 16 Gy for <4 cm)

Results: Better pain response and LC with SBRT On subanalysis, only the 16 Gy group had significant response 2-wk CR/PR 62% vs. 36% 3-mos CR/PR 72% vs. 49% 9-mos CR/PR 77% vs. 46% Similar adverse effects and QOL

Interpretation: In this phase II study, single fx SBRT to bone metastases is noinferior to 30 Gy/10 fx by the prespecified study design. The differences in results seem to suggest SBRT is superior.

RTOG 9714

Bone · Fractionation

Hartsell et al, JNCI, 2005 · N=898

Population: Breast or prostate bone metastasis, 1-3 sites, moderate to severe pain

Methods: ➜ 8 Gy vs. ➜ 30 Gy/10 fx

Results: CR 15% vs. 18%, NS PR 50% vs .48%, NS At 3 mos, 33% no longer needed narcotics Re-treatment 18% vs 9% in single males Grade 2-4 adverse effects 10% vs 17%

Interpretation: Single fx and 10 fx RT were equivalent at 3 months with few adverse effects. There were retreatments with single fx arm but less adverse effects.

RTOG 7402

Bone · Fractionation

Tong et al, Cancer, 1982 · N=459

Population: Painful bony mets, no prior RT to site. Stratified single mets and multiple mets (with one painful site)

Methods: Single met: 40.5 Gy/15 fx vs. 20/5 Multiple mets: 30/10 vs. 15/5 vs. 20/5 vs. 25/5.

Results: 90% achieved pain relief, 54% complete relief. Fractionation did not affect the frequency of relief. Of those reporting pain relief, in 96% this occurred within 4 weeks

Interpretation: Low-dose short-course RT is equivalent to long-course for pain relief.

MSK-18-196 ⭐️

Bone · Prophylactic RT

Gillespie et al, JCO, 2023 · N=78

Population: high risk bone lesion: bulky size ≥2 cm, junctional spine or posterior element, hip or sacroiliac joint, long bone with 1/3-2/3 cortical thickness

Methods: ➜ 🏆 prophylactic RT vs. ➜ standard of care Endpoint: SRE (skeletal related event: fracture, cord compression, surgery, or later pain requiring RT)

Results: ✅ 1-yr SRE 2% vs. 29% 1-yr SRE (excluding RT) 0% vs. 11% Hospitalizations for SRE n=0 vs 4 Pain also reduced with RT Median OS 1.7 years vs. 1.1 years Most events in junctional spine or bulky disease

Interpretation: Prophylactic radiation to high risk bony lesions reduced skeletal related events. In this phase II study, OS showed improvement.

University Medical Center Utrecht, the Netherlands

Bone · Reirradiation

Huisman et al, IJROBP, 2012 · N=527

Population: Bone metastases treated with repeat RT, most with single fx, after initial dose of 4-8 Gy x 1

Methods: Meta-analysis of 7 trials

Results: Pain response with reirradiation 58% Subtantial heterogeneity between studies

Interpretation: Reirradiation is effective for pain relief but ~40% do not benefit.

Dutch Dexa

Bone · Dexamethasone

van der Linden et al, IJROBP, 2020 · N=295

Population: bone metastasis with pain

Methods: ➜ 8 mg dex prior to RT, then daily x3 vs. ➜ 8 mg dex prior to RT, then placebo x3 vs. ➜ placebo x4

Results: pain flair 38% vs. 27% vs. 39%, p=0.07 But flairs in group B did not return to baseline pain flair duration 2.7 vs. 4.5 vs. 3.3 days, p=0.567 pain flair delay by 2-5 days 52% vs. 73% vs. 99% Arm 1 had low scores on days 2-5

Interpretation: Dexamethasone did not reduce the incidence of pain flairs significantly. The power of the study may be too low for three arms. Dexamethasone did lead to delayed pain flare and lower scores on day 2-5.

University of Toronto

Bone · Dexamethasone

Chow et al, Lancet Oncol, 2015 · N=298

Population: bone metastasis with pain

Methods: 8 mg dexamethasone at least 1 hour before start of RT (dose single 8 Gy in all) then every day for 4 days after RT vs. placebo

Results: pain flares improved with dexamethasone: 26% vs. 35%, p=0.05

Interpretation: Dexamethasone reduces radiation induced pain flare.

MSKCC

Oligometastasis · SBRT

Zelefsky et al, IJROBP, 2020 · N=117

Population: 1-5 oligometastases

Methods: ➜ 24 Gy single fx SBRT vs. ➜ 27 Gy/3 fx SBRT

Results: 2-yr LR 3% vs. 9% 3-yr LR 6% vs. 22%, p=0.0048 3-yr DM 5% vs. 22%, p=0.10

Interpretation: Single fraction SBRT to 24 Gy for oligometastasis provides better local control than 27 Gy in 3 fractions.

University of Kentucky Medical Center

Spine · Benefit of surgery

Patchell et al, Lancet, 2005 · N=101

Population: Spinal cord compression at one site caused by metastatic solid tumor. Cannot have been paraplegic for >48 hours. Expected survival of 3 months

Methods: ➜ Surgery with 30 Gy RT vs. ➜ 30 Gy RT alone Endpoint: ability to walk 4 steps Most surgery was anterior approach

Results: •Surgery improves ability to walk •Ability to walk 84% vs. 57%, 122 days vs. 13 days •If able to walk at entry, 94% vs. 74% ambulating, 153 vs. 54 days •If unable to walk at entry, 62% vs. 19% regained ability to walk, 59 vs. 0 days •Surgical group had lower need for coricosteroids and narcotics

Interpretation: Laminectomy plus RT improves ability to walk over RT alone.

University Hospital Schleswig-Holstein, Luebeck, Germany

Spine · Nomogram

Rades et al, IJROBP, 2008 · N=2096

Population: Metastatic spinal cord compression

Methods: Nomogram developed to predict ambulatory function after RT and validated in a prospective trial.

Results: Refer to publication for useful nomogram to predict ability to walk and survival. For outcomes use scoring system that is validated in 2011 publication. There was a benefit to laminectomy plus stabilization followed by RT with all groups combined. Addition of laminectomy did not provide benefit compared to RT alone. Important factors: primary tumor type, interval between tumor diagnosis and MSCC, visceral metastases, motor function before RT, and time developing motor deficits before RT

Interpretation: This nomogram helps guide treatment opions including best supportive care, surgery, and radiation

SCORAD III

Spine · Single fraction vs. Short course RT

Hoskin et al, JAMA, 2019 · N=686

Population: Metastatic epidural cord compression

Methods: ➜ Single fraction 8 Gy x 1 vs. ➜ Short course 4 Gy x 5 Primary endpoint ambulatory status (AS) at week 8 Secondary endpoints: weeks 1, 4, and 12

Results: •8-week AS 69% vs. 73% (did not meet predefined noninferiority in CI) •Secondary AS at weeks 1, 4, and 12 were noninferior •12-wk OS 50-55%, not different

Interpretation: Single fraction vs. short course RT for cord compression was not found to be noninferior at the primary endpoint of 8 weeks. However secondary endpoints at week 1, 4, and 12 were noninferior. Altogether the results are similar and suggest effectiveness of the single fraction regimen.

SCORE-2 (AR0 2009/01)

Spine · Short vs Long course RT

Rades et al, JCO, 2016 Rades et al, IJROBP, 2019 · N=203

Population: Metastatic epidural cord compression

Methods: ➜ Short course 4 Gy x 5 vs. ➜ Long course 3 Gy x 10

Results: •Patient reported pain relief and distress were noninferior at 1, 3, and 6 months •ORR not different, 87% short vs 90% (NS) •No difference in ambulatory rates, improvement, no further progression, or deterioration 6-mo OS 42% vs 38% (NS) 6-mo local PFS 75% vs. 82% (NS)

Interpretation: Short course 5 fx RT to 20 Gy is noninferior to long course 10 fx to 30 Gy in metastatic cord compression in pain relief, distress, ambulatory rates, improvement, no further progression, or deterioration.

University of Germany, Lubeck

Spine · Short vs Long course RT

Rades et al, IJROBP, 2011 · N=265

Population: Metastatic spinal cord compression

Methods: ➜ Short course 8 Gy x 1 or 4 Gy x 5 vs. ➜ Long course 3 Gy x 10 or 2.5 Gy x 15 or 2Gy x 20

Results: 1-yr LC 61% vs 81% 1-yr OS 23% vs 30%, p=0.28 motor function improvement 37-39% (NS)

Interpretation: Long course regimens lead to improved LC for cord compression compared to short course. Good prognosis patients should be considered for long course RT.

RTOG 0631

Spine · SBRT spine

Ryu et al, JAMA Oncol, 2023 · N=353

Population: 1-3 spine metastasis, localized, NRPS score of ≥5

Methods: Phase III ➜ SBRT 16 or 18 Gy single fx (as low as 80% coverage permitted) vs. ➜ EBRT 8 Gy single fx

Results: Pain response seemed better with EBRT, 41% vs. 61%, p=0.99 (one-side p test), and p=0.01 for 2-sided p No difference in adverse effects

Interpretation: Single fraction SBRT and EBRT to spine show no difference in pain response or adverse effects.

CCTG SC.24/ TROG 17.0

Spine · SBRT vs. conventional

Sahgal et al, Lancet Oncol, 2021 Zeng et al, IJROBP, 2022 · N=229

Population: Painful spine metastasis, ≤3 consecutive vertebra (oligo not required)

Methods: Phase III ➜ 🏆 SBRT 24 Gy/ 2 fx vs. ➜ conventional 20 Gy/ 5 fx

Results: ✅ 3-mo pain CR 35% vs. 14% ✅ 2-yr LF 15% vs. 36% Iatrogenic vertebral fractures n=8/12 vs. 4/12 All grade 3 vertebral fractions were with SBRT (n=5/12)

Interpretation: SBRT to 24 Gy in 2 fractions leads to better CR in pain response than conventional RT in 5 fractions.

CCTG HE1

Liver · Liver palliation

Dawson et al, Lancet Oncol, 2024 · N=66

Population: refractory HCC or liver metastasis, ECOG 0-3, life expectancy ≥3 mos, pain score 4-10/10 and stable

Methods: ➜ best supportive care vs. ➜ 8 Gy single fraction with zofran and dexamethasone

Results: 1-mo improvement of hepatic pain by 2 points in 22% vs. 67% 1-mo grade 3-4 adverse effects abdominal pain 3% vs. 9% ascities 3% vs. 6%

Interpretation: Single fraction palliative radiation therapy to liver improves pain scores.

TROG 13.01 SAFFRON II

Lung · Oligometastasis dose

Siva et al, JAMA Oncol, 2021 · N=90

Population: pulmonary oligometastasis (133 lesions)

Methods: ➜ 28 Gy single fx SBRT vs. ➜ 48 Gy/ 4 fx SBRT

Results: 3-yr FFLF 64% vs. 80%, p=0.13 Higher LF in colorectral tumors 3-yr OS 67-81%%, not different 3-yr FFDM 37% vs. 32%, not different more immune activation with single fx

Interpretation: In pulmonary metastasis, single fraction radiation is favored. This method leads to no difference in LC, OS, or DFS, and additionally activates the immune system better then multi-fraction RT.

IAEA

Lung · Lung palliation

Jeremic et al, ASTRO, 2017 Rathod et al, ASTRO, 2017 · N=185

Population: Advanced NSCLC stage wet IIIB and IV

Methods: ➜ platinum chemo x3 vs. ➜ low fraction RT (10 Gy/ 1 fx or 16 Gy/ 2 fx with one wk split) followed by same chemo

Results: •RT improved Global HRQoL, role and social functioning, pain, dyspnea, N/V, and lung specific symptoms •QOL improved at 2 weeks and was maintained With ITT, no difference in OS Median OS 6.5 vs. 5.8 mos 1-yr OS 33% vs. 19 % 2-yr OS 16% vs. 6% (p=0.19) 1-yr LRPFS 20% vs. 12% 2-yr LFPRFS 9% vs. 3% (p=0.52) Acute grade >3 adverse effects similar and infrequent

Interpretation: Short course thoracic RT prior to chemo improves QOL. Effect is sustained over time. No impact on palliative RT in outcomes or adverse effects.

Cross Cancer Institute, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada

Lung · Palliative RT dose

Fairchild et al, JCO, 2008 · N=3473

Population: 13 randomized trials comparing two or more dose fractionation schemes

Methods: 13 randomized trials comparing two or more dose fractionation schemes. Random effects model used

Results: High dose RT associated with improved OS and symptom control. Total symptom control 65% low dose vs 77% high dose RT For individual symptoms, results comparable Symptom control improved with BED10 >35Gy 1-yr OS 22% low dose vs 27% high dose Low dose with 1.2-fold chance of repeat RT Dysphagia greated with high dose RT

Interpretation: Higher dose RT was associated with better OS. Doses of BED10 >35Gy may be warranted but should weighed against adverse effects and time.

Norwegian LCSG

Lung · Addition of RT to chemo

Strøm et al, Br J Cancer, 2013 · N=191

Population: Stage III NSCLC with poor prognostic factors and otherwise curative

Methods: ➜ carbo/vinorelbin x4 vs. ➜ same with 42 Gy/15 starting at 2nd cycle

Results: OS improved with chemoRT Median OS 9.7 mos vs. 12.6 chemoRT 1-yr OS 53% vs. 34% HRQOL declined during treatment with chemoRT but then remained unchanged

Interpretation: ChemoRT improved OS compared to chemo alone in Stage III patients not eligible for curative therapy.

University of Warmia and Mazury, Poland

Lung · Addition of chemo to RT

Nawrocki et al, J Thorac Oncol, 2010 · N=99

Population: NSCLC Stage IIIA and IIIB not eligible for radical treatment: tumor >8cm and/or FEV≤40%, ECOG 0-2, tumor related chest symptoms

Methods: ➜ 30 Gy/10 fx alone vs. ➜ cis/vinorelbine x2 then same RT with concurrent chemo x1 cycle

Results: OS and PFS improved with chemoRT Median OS 9.0 mos RT vs. 12.9 chemoRT No deaths during tx with RT alone, six with chemoRT PFS 4.7 mos vs. 7.3 mos Response rate 27% vs. 53% (NS)

Interpretation: ChemoRT improved OS and PFS compared to RT alone in Stage III patients not eligible for curative therapy.
