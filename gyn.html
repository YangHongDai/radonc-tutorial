🌸 GYNECOLOGIC CANCERS

婦科腫瘤

子宮頸、子宮內膜、外陰癌的放射治療原則。

Radiation for cervical, endometrial, and vulvar cancers.

子宮頸癌

局部晚期子宮頸癌

標準治療: concurrent chemoRT (weekly cisplatin 40 mg/m² × 5–6) + 近接治療 boost。

近接治療: HDR / LDR，MRI-guided IGABT (EMBRACE-I) 提升 LC 與降低毒性。

目標劑量: HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2 (α/β=10)。

INTERLACE: Induction chemo → chemoRT 相較 chemoRT alone 提升 PFS 與 OS。

Standard: Concurrent chemoRT (weekly cisplatin 40 mg/m² × 5–6) + brachytherapy boost.

Brachytherapy: HDR / LDR; MRI-guided IGABT (EMBRACE-I) improves LC and reduces toxicity.

Target: HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2 (α/β=10).

INTERLACE: Induction chemo → chemoRT improves PFS and OS vs chemoRT alone.

子宮內膜癌

子宮內膜癌

PORTEC-1/2: 中風險 endometrioid 子宮內膜癌，vaginal brachytherapy (VBT) 對局部復發相當於 EBRT 且毒性較低。

PORTEC-3: 高風險 endometrial (Stage III / serous / clear cell) — chemoRT vs RT alone: chemoRT 提升 OS 於 Stage III。

GOG 249: 高中風險 — VBT + chemo 沒有比 pelvic RT 好。

PORTEC-1/2: Intermediate-risk endometrioid — vaginal brachytherapy (VBT) equivalent to EBRT for local recurrence with less toxicity.

PORTEC-3: High-risk endometrial (Stage III / serous / clear cell) — chemoRT vs RT alone: OS benefit in Stage III.

GOG 249: High-intermediate risk — VBT + chemo not superior to pelvic RT.

外陰癌

外陰癌

手術為主；≥ 2 陽性淋巴結或 ECE 需輔助 RT。GOG 205 顯示 chemoRT (5-FU/cisplatin) 63.6% CR。

Surgery-first; adjuvant RT for ≥ 2 positive nodes or ECE. GOG 205 — chemoRT (5-FU/cisplatin) yields 63.6% CR.

關鍵試驗

關鍵臨床試驗

以下為此主題的核心隨機試驗與樞紐研究，資料源自 Estes 2025 Key Studies 資料表，已整合於本章。

Core randomized trials and pivotal studies for this topic, from the Estes 2025 Key Studies tables, are integrated into this chapter.

University of Milan, Italy

Cervical · Surgery vs. RT

Landoni et al, Lancet, 1997 Landoni et al, J Gynecol Oncol, 2017 · N=343

Population: Stage IB + IIA cervical cancer

Methods: ➜ Radical hyst + LND + adjuvant RT as indicated vs. ➜ 47 Gy EBRT + LDR to 70-90 Gy (median 76) Adjuvant EBRT to 50.4 Gy for ≥pT2b, ≤3 mm cervical stroma margin, cut-through, or N+ (given in 64%) EFRT 45 Gy given if PA or common iliac N+

Results: ↔️ No difference in 5-yr OS (83%), 20-yr OS (~75%), DFS (74%), or recurrence (25%). Median time to relapse 13.5 vs. 11.5 mos (p=0.10) 20-yr late complications 32% vs. 23%, worse with surgery. 64% in surgery arm had adjuvant RT Worst morbidity with adjuvant RT plus surgery (hydronephrosis, GU incontinence, LE edema, bowel obstruction) ⬆️ OS benefit trend with RT in size ≥4 cm, and significant benefit in size ≥5 cm ⬆️ OS benefit trend with surgery in adeno, p=0.09 65% received adjuvant RT

Interpretation: Surgery and RT have similar efficacy. Adverse effects are worse with surgery. RT seems preferable for larger tumors and surgery seems preferable for adenocarcinoma. Tumor diameter, histology, age, and comorbidities should be considered.

EORTC 55994

Cervical · Neoadjuvant chemo + surgery vs. chemoRT

Kenter et al, JCO, 2023 · N=626

Population: Cervix SCC Stage IB2, IIA, IIB

Methods: ➜ neoadjuvant cisplatin based chemo, radical hysterectomy, & post-op chemoRT as indicated for N+, parametrial invasion, or +M (no RT for Sedlis criteria) vs. ➜ 🏆 definitive RT + cisplatin

Results: 5-yr OS not different, 72-76% 5-yr PFS 57% vs. 66%, p=0.010 Post-op chemoRT was given in 48% late grade ≥3 effects 15% vs. 21%, worse with radiation

Interpretation: Neoadjuvant chemotherapy followed by surgery is not superior to chemoradiation. PFS may be worse.

Tata Memorial Centre, Mumbai

Cervical · Neoadjuvant chemo + surgery vs. chemoRT

Gupta et al, JCO, 2018 · N=635

Population: Cervix SCC Stage IB2, IIA, IIB

Methods: ➜ neoadjuvant carbo/taxol, radical hysterectomy, & post-op RT or chemo as indicated vs. ➜ 🏆 definitive RT + cisplatin

Results: No change in OS. Worse DFS with neoadjuvant 5-yr DFS 69% neoadjuvant vs. 78% 5-yr OS 75% both arms delayed adverse effects: rectal 2.2% vs. 3.5%, bladder 1.6% vs. 3.5%, vaginal 12.0% vs. 26%

Interpretation: Neoadjuvant chemotherapy before hysterectomy should not be employed. DFS was worsened compared to definitive chemoRT.

GOG 71

Cervical · Definitive RT vs Neoadjuvant RT + surgery

Keys et al, Gynecol Oncol, 2003 · N=256

Population: Bulky IB disease (tumor or cervix ≥4cm)

Methods: ➜ 🏆 40 Gy EBRT + 40 Gy LDR vs. ➜ 45 Gy EBRT + 30 Gy LDR + extrafascial hysterectomy

Results: 5-yr LR 27% vs. 14% Hyst 5-yr PFS 53% vs. 62%, p=0.09 Disease progression 46% vs. 37%, p=0.07 Grade 3-4 effects not different On unplanned subanalysis hyst improved control and survival in size 4-6 cm

Interpretation: There was no overall benefit to hysterectomy after definitive RT. On subanalysis hysterectomy seemed to be favored in larger tumors of size 4-6 cm.

ENGOT-cx11/GOG-3047/KEYNOTE-A18

Cervical · concurrent and adjuvant pembrolizumab

Lorusso et al, Lancet, 2024 · N=1060

Population: Cervix SCC Stage FIGO IB2-IIB node positive, or III-IVA

Methods: ➜ Definitive chemoRT vs. ➜ 🏆 Definitive chemoRT + concurrent and adjuvant pembrolizumab 45-50 Gy WPRT plus brachy to 25-30 Gy in 4-6 fractions HDR or LDR

Results: 2-yr OS 81% vs. 87%, not different 2-yr PFS 57% vs. 68% Median PFS not reached in either arm

Interpretation: Concurrent and adjuvant pembrolizumab with chemoradiation improves PFS in locally advanced cervical cancer.

INTERLACE

Cervical · Induction chemo then chemoRT vs chemo RT

McCormack et al, Lancet, 2024 · N=500

Population: Cervix SCC Stage FIGO IB1, IB2, II, IIIB, IVA Excluded: periaortic nodes or lower vaginal involvement

Methods: ➜ definitive RT + cisplatin vs. ➜ 🏆 neoadjuvant carbo/taxol x6 then definitive RT + cisplatin 40-50.4 Gy/ 20-28 fx plus brachy to 78-86 Gy EQD2

Results: 5-yr OS 72% vs. 80% 3-yr LRR 9% vs. 11% 3-yr DM only 7% vs. 12% 5-yr PFS 64% vs. 72% Grade ≥3 adverse effects 48% vs. 49% (mainly heme)

Interpretation: Induction chemotherapy prior to definitive chemoradiation improves OS and PFS.

GOG 123

Cervical · Neoadjuvant ChemoRT then surgery

Keys et al, NEJM, 1999 Stehman et al, Am J Obstet Gynecol, 2007 · N=369

Population: Cervical cancer ≥4 cm (previously IB2, later staged as IB3)

Methods: ➜ neoadjuvant definitive WPRT+brachy→simple hyst vs. ➜ 🏆 neoadjuvant definitive WPRT+brachy+weekly cisplatin→hyst

Results: 3-yr OS 83% vs. 74% 3-yr LR 21% vs. 37% 5-yr PFS 71% vs. 60% 5-yr OS 78% vs. 64% pCR 52% vs. 41%

Interpretation: The addition of chemotherapy to RT prior to hysterectomy improves OS and LC.

NCIC

Cervical · Definitive CRT

Pearcey et al, JCO, 2002 · N=259

Population: IB2-IVA of size >5cm or +N

Methods: ➜ WPRT 45 Gy + brachy (35 Gy LDR or 24 Gy HDR) alone vs. ➜ RT + weekly cisplatin

Results: No significant difference in PFS, 3- or 5-yr OS (66-69% and 58-62%)

Interpretation: The addition of chemotherapy to definitive RT did not improve PFS or OS.

University of Liverpool, Liverpool, UK

Cervical · Definitive CRT

Green et al, Lancet, 2001 · N=3542

Population: Meta-analysis of 19 trials of cervival cancer patients treated with or without chemotherapy

Methods: Meta-analysis of 19 trials. ➜ RT alone vs. ➜ 🏆 RT with concurrent chemo

Results: PFS and OS improved with chemo PFS 47% RT vs. 63% chemoRT OS 40% vs. 52%, greatest in IB-IIB No increase in late adverse effects with RT

Interpretation: The addition of chemotherapy to definitive RT improves OS in this meta-analysis.

GOG 85/SWOG 8695

Cervical · Definitive CRT

Whitney et al, JCO, 1999 · N=368

Population: Stage IIB-IVA, negative PA LN

Methods: ➜ WPRT concurent oral hydroxyurea vs. ➜ 🏆 WPRT concurrent 5-FU/cis

Results: OS and PFS improved with chemo 5FU/cis

Interpretation: 5-FU cisplatin added to definitive RT improves OS and PFS compared to hydroxyurea.

GOG 120

Cervical · Definitive CRT

Rose et al, NEJM, 1999 · N=526

Population: IIB-IVA disease, surgically staged, PA LN negative

Methods: ➜ WPRT + brachy 81 Gy alone vs. ➜ RT + weekly cisplatin vs. ➜ RT + cis 5-FU hydroxyurea vs. ➜ RT+ hydroxyurea

Results: 2-yr PFS 67% for cisplatin alone vs. 64% for combo vs. 47% for hydroxyurea. OS also improved. adverse effects less for cisplatin alone vs. combo.

Interpretation: The addition of chemotherapy to definitive RT improves OS. Cisplatin alone is less toxic than with 5-FU and should be standard.

RTOG 9001

Cervical · Definitive CRT: EFRT vs. WPRT/chemo

Eifel et al, JCO, 2004 · N=386

Population: Stage IIB-IVA, or IB-IIA with tumor ≥ 5cm or + pelvic LN

Methods: ➜ 45 Gy EFRT + LDR to 85 Gy vs. ➜ 🏆 WPRT + LDR to 85 Gy + cisplatin/5-FU

Results: 8-yr OS 41% vs. 67% DFS 46% vs. 61% LRF 35% vs. 18% DM 35% vs. 20% Late effects unchanged.

Interpretation: The addition of chemotherapy to definitive RT improves OS, LR, and DM.

RTOG 9001

Cervical · Definitive CRT: EFRT vs. WPRT/chemo

Eifel et al, JCO, 2004 · N=386

Population: Stage IIB-IVA, or IB-IIA with tumor ≥ 5cm or + pelvic LN

Methods: →45 Gy EFRT + LDR to 85 Gy vs. →🏆 WPRT + LDR to 85 Gy + cisplatin/5-FU

Results: 8-yr OS 41% vs. 67% DFS 46% vs. 61% LRF 35% vs. 18% DM 35% vs. 20% Late effects unchanged.

Interpretation: The addition of chemotherapy to definitive RT improves OS, LR, and DM.

EORTC

Cervical · EFRT vs. pelvic RT

Haei et al, Radiother Oncol, 1988 · N=441

Population: Stage IB-IIB N1 or Stage IIB with distal vaginal or parametrial involvement, or Stage III. PA nodes must be negative.

Methods: ➜ WPRT 45 Gy vs. ➜ EFRT 45 Gy

Results: No change in LC, DFS, or DM. Decreased PA failure noted.

Interpretation: EFRT does not improve LC, DFS, or DM. There was decreased PA failure.

RTOG 7920

Cervical · EFRT vs. pelvic RT

Rotman et al, JAMA, 1995 · N=367

Population: IB-IIA ≥ 4cm, or IIB

Methods: ➜ WPRT 40-50 Gy vs. ➜ 🏆 EFRT 44-45 Gy → 30-40 Gy intracavitary brachytherapy to point A

Results: Benefit in OS and DM with EFRT 10-yr OS 44% vs. 55% EFRT DM 23% vs. 16% DFS ~42%, not different LRF 31-35%, not different Local salvage 8% vs. 25% Grade 4-5 adverse effects 4% vs. 8% (p=0.06)

Interpretation: EFRT has improved OS over WPRT with less DM and salvage. DFS and LRF are unchanged.

AC Camargo Cancer Center, São Paulo, Brazil

Cervical · EFRT vs. pelvic RT

Sapienza et al, Gynecol Oncol, 2016 · N=1000

Population: Meta-analysis to evaluate EFRT

Methods: 4 studies (RTOG 7920, EORTC, Osaka University, Chang Gung University)

Results: EFRT improved PA failure (HR 0.35), DM (HR 0.69) LRC and CSM were not different

Interpretation: EFRT reduces PA failure and DM. CSM was unchanged.

OUTBACK

Cervical · Adjuvant "outback" chemo after definitive RT

Mileshkin et al, Lancet Oncol, 2023 · N=919

Population: locally advanced cervical cancer (FIGO 2008 stage IB1 and node positive, IB2, II, IIIB or IVA)

Methods: ➜ 🏆 Definitive chemoRT vs. ➜ Definitive chemoRT with adjuvant carbo/taxol -RT: 45-50.4Gy EBRT + brachy -Adjuvant: q3w x4 carbo (AUC5)/taxol (155) mg/m2 -Concurrent: weekly cisplatin 40 mg/m2

Results: No benefit to adjuvant chemo 5-yr OS 72%, not different 5-yr PFS 62%, not different 5-yr DM 14%, not different No benefit even if treatment completed

Interpretation: Adjuvant chemo after definitive chemoRT should not be employed. OS or PFS are not improved with the addition of adjuvant chemo compared to chemoRT alone.

NCI, Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México, México City, México

Cervical · Adjuvant "outback" chemo after definitive RT

Duenas-Gonzales et al, JCO, 2011 · N=515

Population: Stage IIB-IVA

Methods: ➜ EBRT 50.4 Gy + brachy 30-35 Gy with weekly cisplatin vs. ➜ Same RT + concurrent cis/gem + "outback" cis/gem

Results: Improved PFS with chemo 3-yr PFS 74% chemo vs. 65% worse grade 3/4 effects: 87% vs. 46% More hospitalizations 30% vs. 11%

Interpretation: Concurrent cis/gem with "outback" cis/gem improved PFS compared to standard definitive RT, but adverse effects was markedly increased.

GOG 263/ KGOG 1008

Cervical · Adjuvant chemoRT in int risk after surgery

Ryu et al, Ann Oncol, 2025 · N=340

Population: cervical cancer stage I-IIA with: •LVI+ and ≥1 of: deep third stromal involvement, middle 1/3 and size ≥2 cm, superficial 1/3 and size ≥5 cm •LVI- with middle or deep involvement and size ≥4 cm

Methods: ➜ WPRT 50.4 Gy vs. ➜ WPRT with concurrent carbo/taxol weekly x6 cycles

Results: 3-yr RFS 85% vs. 89%, p=0.09 3-yr OS 90% vs. 97%, p=0.07

Interpretation: There was no statistically significant improvement in RFS or OS with concurrent chemotherapy in intermediate risk post-op cervical cancer. There was however trend to improvement in RFS and OS, which could support concurrent chemotherapy, especially in those with multiple risk factors.

RTOG 0724

Cervical · Further chemo after adjuvant CRT

Jhingran, ASCO, 2024 · N=285

Population: Cervix cancer stage IA2, IB or IIA squamous s/p radical hysterectomy with positive nodes, positive parametrium, positive paraaortic nodes (completely resected and PET negative after surgery)

Methods: 45-50.4 Gy + cisplatin → ➜ no further treatment vs. ➜ additional carbo/paclitaxel VC brachy allowed

Results: 4-yr DFS 76-77%, not different 4-yr OS 87%, not different

Interpretation: There is no benefit to additional chemotherapy after adjuvant concurrernt chemoradiation in post-op cervical cancer with high risk features.

STARS

Cervical · Adjuvant chemoRT after surgery

Huang et al, JAMA Oncol, 2021 · N=1048

Population: cervical cancer stage IB-IIA with ≥1 of LVSI, deep stromal invasion, N+, positive margin, or parametrial invasion

Methods: ➜ WPRT 45-50 Gy vs. ➜ WPRT with concurrent cisplatin vs. ➜ 🏆 WPRT with sandwich cisplatin and taxol Sequential: 2 cycles pre and 2 cycles post RT, q3w cis 60-75 mg/m2 and paclitaxel 135-175 mg/m2 Concurrent: weekly cisplatin 30-40 mg/m2 Neoadj chemo allowed for size >4 cm

Results: DFS, cancer death, and OS significantly improved with sequential vs. RT alone. DFS improved with sequential vs. concurrent WPRT vs. WPRT con vs. WPRT seq: 3-yr DFS 82% vs. 85% vs. 90% High risk (+margin, +node) (supp eFig 3) 3-yr DFS 67% vs. 68% vs. 83% 3-yr OS 78% vs. 80% vs. 90% Int risk only (eFig 3) 3-yr DFS 85% vs. 92% vs. 90% 3-yr OS 90% vs. 94% vs. 90% results were maintained across subgroups except for size >4 cm or received neoadjuvant chemo 5-yr cancer death 11% vs. 10% vs. 8% 5-yr OS 88% vs. 89% vs. 92% 5-yr DM 11% vs. 11% vs. 7%

Interpretation: Sequential chemotherapy and radiation has superior DFS and risk of cancer death than radiation alone. On subanalysis, in the high risk group only sandwich chemo gave benefit in DFS and OS. In the intermediate risk group, concurrent and sandwich chemo both gave benefit in DFS and OS. Sequential also had superior DFS compared to concurrent.

GOG post-op nomogram

Cervical · Adjuvant RT after surgery

Levinson et al, Gynecol Oncol, 2021 · N=715

Population: Stage I cervix s/p surgery and no chemo from GOG 49, 92, and 141

Methods: Ancillary analysis of GOG 49, 92, and 141 to determine risk of recurrence after surgery

Results: See publication for nomograms for 3-yr recurrence in SCC and ACC after surgery For SCC, DOI led to the most risk For ACC, tumor size and LVI were the biggest factors

Interpretation: A nomogram was developed to determine the risk of recurrence after surgery in Stage I cervical cancer.

"Sedlis trial" GOG 92

Cervical · Adjuvant RT after surgery

Sedlis et al, Gynecol Oncol, 1999 Rotman et al, IJROBP, 2006 · N=277

Population: Stage IB, N0 s/p radical hysterectomy with ≥2 risk factors of >1/3 stromal invasion, LVI, size ≥4 cm

Methods: ➜ 🏆 WPRT 46 Gy or 50.4 Gy vs. ➜ obs Primary endpoint: PFS Not powered for OS

Results: 10-yr LR 14% vs. 21% 10-yr PFS 78% vs. 65% 10-yr OS 80% vs. 71%, p=0.07 12-yr recurrence adenocarcinoma or adenosquamous 8.8% vs. 44% obs acute grade 3 & 4 effects 7% vs. 2% late adverse effects not recorded

Interpretation: Adjuvant RT improves PFS and LR in int risk IB cervical cancer.

"Peters trial" GOG 109/SWOG 8797/RTOG 9112/INT 0107

Cervical · Adjuvant chemoRT after surgery

Peters et al, JCO, 2000 · N=243

Population: Stage IA2, IB, IIA s/p radical hysterectomy with ≥1 of node positive, positive margin or parametrial involvement

Methods: ➜ WPRT 49.3 Gy/ 29 fx vs. ➜ 🏆 WPRT + x2 cycles concurrent cis/5-FU and x2 adjuvant cis/5FU x4 If common iliac node+, 45 Gy/ 1.5 Gy daily to PA nodes given

Results: 4-yr OS 71% vs. 81% 4-yr PFS 63% vs. 80% No difference in recurrence patterns 60% completed all 4 cycles of chemo grade 4 adverse effects in 4% vs. 17% grade 5 in n=1 in CRT arm, but this patient declined chemo

Interpretation: Adjuvant cisplatin improves OS and PFS in resected cervical cancer with + nodes, margin, or parametrial involvement.

Washington University

Cervical · PET positive nodes outcome

Kidd et al, JCO, 2010 · N=560

Population: Patients with cervical cancer who underwent pre-op PET

Methods: Retrospective analysis

Results: -Periaortic nodes long term 4-5-yr DSS of ~20-40% (see figure) -SCV nodes long term 4-5-yr DSS of ~10-35% (see figure) -(Refer to paper for RFS and DSS plots for pelvic, periaortic, and SCV nodes per FIGO stage [Kaplan-Meier and HRs provided, not absolute values]) Risk of pelvic and PA nodes on PET: Stage I: 9-51% and 0-9% Stage IIA: 50%, 21% Stage IIB: 54%, 17% Stage IIIA 50%, 25% Stage IIIB/IV 55-85%, 13-38% Frequency of lymph node metastases was similar to historic surgical series

Interpretation: PET detected nodes help predict DSS. Long term DSS is possible in advanced stages, even with supraclavicular metastasis.
