子宮頸癌基本概念、流行病學、危險因子、篩檢與預防

子宮頸癌是全球女性第 4 常見惡性腫瘤。美國的發生率因為子宮頸抹片篩檢與人類乳突病毒疫苗接種而下降。診斷中位年齡約 50 歲，最常診斷於 35–44 歲女性。最常見病理型態是鱗狀上皮細胞癌。

  • 主要危險因子：人類乳突病毒感染；超過 90–95% 病例與人類乳突病毒相關，其中第 16 型與第 18 型約占 70%；其他高危險型包括 31、33、45、52、58 型。
  • 族群差異：黑人女性與美洲原住民女性的死亡率高於白人女性。
  • 其他危險因子：吸菸、免疫功能低下，例如愛滋病或器官移植後免疫抑制、初次性行為年齡較早、多重性伴侶、多產、低社經地位，以及胎兒時期暴露於己烯雌酚。
  • 己烯雌酚暴露：與子宮頸或陰道透明細胞癌相關。
  • 臨床表現：常常沒有症狀，多由篩檢發現；若出現症狀，常見為異常陰道分泌物、性交後出血、性交疼痛、骨盆疼痛。

Cervical cancer is the fourth most common malignancy in women worldwide. In the United States, incidence has declined because of cervical cytology screening and human papillomavirus vaccination. The median age at diagnosis is about 50 years, and it is most often diagnosed in women aged 35–44 years. The most common histology is squamous cell carcinoma.

  • Main risk factor: human papillomavirus infection; more than 90–95% of cases are associated with human papillomavirus, with types 16 and 18 accounting for about 70%. Other high-risk types include 31, 33, 45, 52, and 58.
  • Population disparity: mortality is higher among Black women and Native American women than among White women.
  • Other risk factors: smoking, immunosuppression such as HIV infection or post-transplant immunosuppression, early age at first sexual intercourse, multiple sexual partners, multiparity, low socioeconomic status, and in utero diethylstilbestrol exposure.
  • Diethylstilbestrol exposure: associated with clear cell carcinoma of the cervix or vagina.
  • Presentation: often asymptomatic and detected by screening; symptoms may include abnormal vaginal discharge, postcoital bleeding, dyspareunia, and pelvic pain.

子宮頸解剖、淋巴引流、病理、診斷與 2018 國際婦產科聯盟分期

子宮頸是子宮下段，呈圓柱狀。子宮頸內管由柱狀上皮覆蓋，子宮頸外部由鱗狀上皮覆蓋。鱗柱狀上皮交界處位於外子宮口，是最常發生癌化的位置。主韌帶將子宮頸連接到骨盆側壁，闊韌帶將子宮連接到骨盆側壁，子宮薦骨韌帶則將子宮下段連接到薦骨。

  • 淋巴引流：沿主韌帶、闊韌帶與子宮薦骨韌帶進入區域淋巴系統，包括薦前、閉孔、內髂、外髂、總髂與主動脈旁淋巴結。
  • 遠端轉移：常見位置為肺、肝、骨，也可經胸管轉移至鎖骨上淋巴結。
  • 基本檢查：病史與理學檢查，特別是婦科病史、骨盆檢查、腹部檢查，並檢查鎖骨上與鼠蹊部淋巴結。
  • 實驗室：全血球計數、肝腎功能與電解質，可考慮人類免疫缺乏病毒檢測與懷孕檢查。
  • 診斷程序：陰道鏡下子宮頸切片或子宮頸環狀電切；若切片不足，應做冷刀錐狀切除。
  • 晚期評估：若懷疑侵犯膀胱或直腸，可在麻醉下進行檢查，並合併膀胱鏡與直腸乙狀結腸鏡，必要時放置輸尿管支架。
  • 影像：骨盆核磁共振加顯影並使用陰道凝膠可評估局部侵犯範圍；正子斷層掃描可評估淋巴結與遠端轉移。

The cervix is the lower cylindrical portion of the uterus. The endocervical canal is covered by columnar epithelium, while the ectocervix is covered by squamous epithelium. The squamocolumnar junction at the external cervical os is the most common site of malignant transformation. The cardinal ligaments connect the cervix to the pelvic sidewall, the broad ligaments connect the uterus to the pelvic sidewall, and the uterosacral ligaments connect the lower uterus to the sacrum.

  • Lymphatic drainage: along the cardinal, broad, and uterosacral ligaments to regional nodal basins, including presacral, obturator, internal iliac, external iliac, common iliac, and para-aortic lymph nodes.
  • Distant metastases: common sites include lung, liver, and bone; disease may also spread through the thoracic duct to supraclavicular nodes.
  • Basic evaluation: history and physical examination, especially gynecologic history, pelvic examination, abdominal examination, and assessment of supraclavicular and inguinal nodes.
  • Laboratory tests: complete blood count, liver and renal function, electrolytes, and consideration of HIV testing and pregnancy testing.
  • Diagnostic procedures: colposcopic cervical biopsy or loop electrosurgical excision; if the biopsy is inadequate, cold-knife conization should be performed.
  • Advanced-stage evaluation: if bladder or rectal invasion is suspected, examination under anesthesia with cystoscopy and proctosigmoidoscopy may be performed, with ureteral stenting if needed.
  • Imaging: contrast-enhanced pelvic MRI with vaginal gel helps assess local invasion; PET can evaluate nodal and distant disease.

子宮頸癌治療總架構、術後放療、同步化放療與近接治療

早期子宮頸癌，也就是 IA1、IA2、IB1、IB2、IIA1，通常可採手術策略。若病人需要保留生育能力，可考慮冷刀錐狀切除或根除性子宮頸切除。冷刀錐狀切除可完整取得子宮頸外部與內管組織，利於評估切緣；根除性子宮頸切除會移除子宮頸、上段陰道與子宮旁組織，但保留子宮，並放置子宮頸環紮。

  • 不需保留生育能力：可依病灶深度與大小選擇單純子宮切除、改良根除性子宮切除或根除性子宮切除。
  • IB1 以上：若不保留生育能力，通常需要根除性子宮切除加前哨淋巴結或骨盆淋巴結處理。
  • 重要原則：避免病人最後同時接受手術、放療與化療三重治療，因為併發症會明顯增加。
  • 術後外照射劑量：骨盆 45–50.4 Gy，每次 1.8 Gy。
  • 術後照射範圍：骨盆淋巴結、總髂、內髂、外髂、閉孔淋巴結，以及陰道與子宮旁組織。
  • 陰道目標：至少包含陰道頂端下方 3 cm，或閉孔上方 1 cm，以較低位置為準。
  • 陰道殘端追加：若陰道切緣接近或陽性，或原本接受的是非根除性單純子宮切除，可考慮陰道殘端近接治療追加 5 Gy ×2 次；切緣陽性時可用 3 次。

Early cervical cancer, including stages IA1, IA2, IB1, IB2, and IIA1, can often be managed surgically. If fertility preservation is needed, cold-knife conization or radical trachelectomy can be considered. Cold-knife conization provides a complete specimen of the ectocervix and endocervical canal for margin assessment; radical trachelectomy removes the cervix, upper vagina, and parametrial tissue while preserving the uterus, with cervical cerclage placement.

  • No fertility preservation needed: simple hysterectomy, modified radical hysterectomy, or radical hysterectomy can be selected according to depth and tumor size.
  • Stage IB1 or higher: if fertility preservation is not required, radical hysterectomy plus sentinel lymph-node or pelvic lymph-node assessment is usually needed.
  • Key principle: avoid trimodality therapy with surgery, radiotherapy, and chemotherapy whenever possible, because complications increase substantially.
  • Postoperative external-beam dose: pelvic 45–50.4 Gy at 1.8 Gy per fraction.
  • Postoperative target volume: pelvic nodes, common iliac, internal iliac, external iliac, obturator nodes, vagina, and parametrial tissues.
  • Vaginal target: at least 3 cm below the vaginal apex, or 1 cm above the obturator foramen, whichever is more inferior.
  • Vaginal cuff boost: if the vaginal margin is close or positive, or if the patient had a non-radical simple hysterectomy, consider vaginal cuff brachytherapy boost of 5 Gy ×2 fractions; for positive margin, 3 fractions may be used.

The standard treatment for locally advanced cervical cancer is chemoradiotherapy followed by brachytherapy dose escalation. A common approach is pelvic external-beam radiotherapy to 45 Gy in 25 fractions, with involved nodes treated using a simultaneous integrated boost to 55–57.5 Gy, combined with weekly cisplatin 40 mg/m². The brachytherapy goal is cumulative equivalent dose D90 of 85–90 Gy to the high-risk clinical target volume.

  • Brachytherapy evolution: from conventional point-based planning to CT- or MRI-guided three-dimensional volume-based treatment.
  • Point A: 2 cm superior and 2 cm lateral to the external cervical os.
  • Point B: 2 cm superior and 5 cm lateral to the external cervical os.
  • Image-guided adaptive brachytherapy: improves local control and reduces organ-at-risk dose.
  • Overall treatment time: should be kept within 6–8 weeks, ideally ≤49 days, and should avoid exceeding 56 days. Each additional treatment day may reduce local control by about 0.5–1%.

The core logic in cervical cancer is: early-stage patients are managed mainly with surgery, then postoperative RT or postoperative chemoradiotherapy is determined by Sedlis or Peters criteria; locally advanced patients are managed with pelvic intensity-modulated radiotherapy, weekly cisplatin, image-guided brachytherapy, and strict control of overall treatment time.

子宮內膜癌基本概念、危險因子、臨床表現與診斷檢查

子宮內膜癌是美國最常見婦科惡性腫瘤，約占所有癌症的 3.6%。診斷中位年齡為 63 歲，45 歲以下較少見，約 6%。最常見病理是腺癌。主要危險因子是沒有黃體素拮抗的雌激素暴露。黑人與白人女性的發生率相近，但黑人女性死亡率較高，部分與侵襲性病理型態比例較高有關，也與癌症照護取得不平等、治療延遲有關。

Endometrial cancer is the most common gynecologic malignancy in the United States, accounting for about 3.6% of all cancers. The median age at diagnosis is 63 years; diagnosis under age 45 is uncommon, about 6%. The most common histology is adenocarcinoma. The major risk factor is unopposed estrogen exposure. Incidence is similar in Black and White women, but mortality is higher in Black women, partly related to a higher proportion of aggressive histologies, inequitable access to cancer care, and treatment delays.

The uterus can be divided into the cervix/lower uterine segment and the uterine corpus/fundus. The uterine wall includes endometrium, myometrium, and serosa. Endometrial cancer arises from the endometrium, whereas uterine sarcoma arises from the myometrium. The parametrium is the connective tissue surrounding the uterus; adnexa include the ovaries, fallopian tubes, and broad ligaments.

  • Regional lymphatic drainage: bilateral parametrial, obturator, internal iliac, external iliac, common iliac, para-aortic, presacral, and sacral nodes.
  • Special drainage: fundal tumors may occasionally drain directly to para-aortic lymph nodes.
  • Lymphovascular space invasion: should be classified as absent, focal, substantial, or extensive. Pooled analysis of PORTEC-1 and PORTEC-2 showed substantial/extensive lymphovascular space invasion is the strongest independent risk factor for pelvic nodal recurrence.
  • Molecular classification: POLE-mutated, microsatellite instability, copy-number low/p53 wild-type, and copy-number high/p53-mutated.
  • Adverse prognostic factors: older age, high tumor grade, serous or clear cell histology, larger tumor size, deep myometrial invasion, lymphovascular space invasion, nodal involvement, and lower uterine segment involvement.
  • Locoregional recurrence: the vagina is the most common site.

The basic management of endometrial cancer is surgical staging followed by risk-adapted adjuvant therapy. Early-stage patients usually undergo total hysterectomy plus bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy. If there is obvious cervical involvement, modified radical or radical hysterectomy can be considered. High-risk patients may require omental and peritoneal biopsies.

  • Lymph-node dissection: controversial and usually reserved for high-risk patients.
  • Sentinel lymph-node biopsy: increasingly replaces routine pelvic and para-aortic lymph-node dissection; the FIRES study showed sensitivity of 97.2% and negative predictive value of 99.6%.

Cervical involvement requires consideration of tandem plus ring or ovoid applicators; node-positive disease requires external-beam radiotherapy with nodal boost, followed by brachytherapy.

  • Presentation: erythematous or ulcerative vulvar lesion, sometimes with bleeding or pruritus. Inguinal nodes may be palpable or ulcerated.
  • Synchronous cancer: about 20% may have concurrent cervical cancer.
  • Distant metastasis: the lung is the most common site.
  • Workup: complete history and physical examination, including pelvic and rectal examination; complete blood count, liver function, and pregnancy testing when needed; lesion biopsy with human papillomavirus testing; contrast-enhanced pelvic MRI for local extent; PET can be considered in clinically advanced disease.
  • Differential diagnosis: epidermal cyst, Bartholin gland disease, lichen sclerosus, condyloma acuminatum, lentiginous lesions, skin tag, and seborrheic keratosis.
  • Vulvar anatomy: the vulva includes mons pubis, clitoris, labia majora, and labia minora. Clitoral lesions account for about 10–15%; labia majora or labia minora lesions account for more than 70%. Bartholin glands are located in the posterior labia majora and are less commonly involved; the posterior commissure is the posterior fusion of the labia minora.
  • Lymphatic drainage: mainly to inguinal lymph nodes. Nodal risk is proportional to tumor thickness. Pelvic lymph nodes are considered metastatic disease in vulvar cancer; clitoral lesions may drain directly to pelvic nodes.
  • Pathology: about 90% are squamous cell carcinoma. Risky invasion patterns include spray-like or infiltrative invasion, and diffuse invasion. Less common histologies include melanoma and Bartholin gland adenocarcinoma.
  • Pathology report: should include tumor size, location, number of lesions, depth of invasion, histology, grade, margin status, lymphovascular space invasion, nodal sampling method, number of nodes, nodal metastasis size greater than 5 mm or not, and extranodal extension.

  • Postoperative primary-site RT: the main indication is close margin, usually <8 mm, or positive margin. It may also be considered for lymphovascular space invasion, tumor size, depth of invasion, diffuse invasion, or spray-like pathology.
  • UCLA retrospective data: local recurrence risk factors included stage, margin <8 mm, invasion depth >9 mm, tumor thickness >2.5 mm, lymphovascular space invasion, keratinization >25%, and mitoses >10 per high-power field.
  • Faul study: among close or positive margin patients, local recurrence was 58% with observation and 16% with adjuvant RT; in close-margin patients, recurrence fell from 30% to 5%; in positive-margin patients, recurrence fell from 70% to 33%.
  • Dutch study: using 3, 5, and 8 mm cutoffs, tumor-free margin distance itself did not significantly affect local recurrence; differentiated vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia and lichen sclerosus were the truly significant factors. Therefore, in selected early-stage patients, observation may be acceptable if margin is ≥3 mm.
  • Inguinal and pelvic adjuvant RT: main indications include ≥2 positive nodes, fixed or ulcerated nodes, and extranodal extension.
  • GOG 37: node-positive patients were treated with pelvic lymph-node dissection or bilateral pelvic plus inguinal RT. RT improved 2-year groin recurrence from 24% to 5% and improved 2-year overall survival from 54% to 68%; benefit was greatest in patients with ≥2 positive nodes, extranodal extension, fixed or ulcerated nodes, or positive-node ratio ≥20%.
  • SEER analysis: even a single positive node may benefit from adjuvant RT, especially when ≤12 nodes are removed.
  • GOG 88: inguinal RT alone cannot replace appropriate inguinal lymph-node dissection.
  • GROINSS-V-I: in early vulvar squamous cell carcinoma, tumor <4 cm, clinically node-negative disease, and negative sentinel node, inguinofemoral lymph-node dissection can be omitted.
  • GOG 173: supports sentinel lymph-node biopsy as a reasonable alternative; false-negative risk is lower for tumors <4 cm, but higher for tumors 4–6 cm.
  • GROINSS-V-II / GOG 270: if sentinel-node micrometastasis is ≤2 mm, adjuvant RT is a safe alternative, with 2-year groin recurrence 1.6%; for macrometastasis, 50 Gy RT alone produced higher groin recurrence than lymph-node dissection, 22% vs 7%.
