共通原則 / CNS

Pediatric radiation oncology 共通原則、CNS 腫瘤總論與 CSI

第一部分：Pediatric radiation oncology 共通原則

一、Pediatric cancer 的核心邏輯

Pediatric cancer 和 adult oncology 最大差別是：治癒率常常高，但 late toxicity 的代價也非常高。

所以 pediatric RT 的核心不是單純「把 dose 給足」，而是盡量使用 multimodality risk-adapted treatment，在 cure 和 long-term toxicity 之間精準平衡，用最小必要 volume、最小必要 dose，並優先考慮 growth、neurocognition、endocrine、fertility、second malignancy。能用 surgery/CHT 達成 control 時，不要過度 RT；但也不能為了 de-escalation 犧牲 disease control。

這個精神貫穿所有 pediatric files：Medulloblastoma 嘗試降低 CSI dose，但 18 Gy CSI 在 3–7 歲 average-risk 病人造成 EFS/OS 下降；Ependymoma 強調 extent of resection 是最重要 prognostic factor，但 RT 不應因年齡 <3 歲而一律延後；Neuroblastoma 顯示 21.6 Gy 已是 standard，boost gross residual 沒有改善 outcome；Wilms tumor 則要求 RT day 10–14 內啟動，因為 timing 對 local control 很重要。

一、Pediatric CNS tumors epidemiology

Primary CNS tumors 佔所有 pediatric malignancies 約 15–20%，是兒童第二常見惡性腫瘤，僅次於 hematologic malignancies；同時也是最常見 pediatric solid organ tumor，並且是 0–14 歲兒童癌症死亡的 leading cause。Pediatric CNS tumors 更常是 infratentorial，約 60–70%，也較常是 low-grade。

五、CSI technique

CSI simulation：通常 supine，shoulders down，neck extended，盡量減少 spine curvature；用 5-point mask 固定 head/shoulders，body 用 large vac-loc，必要時 knee sponge 減少 lordosis。Contours 包括 whole brain，必須 cover cribriform plate/crista galli；spine 要包含 entire thecal sac，通常到 S1–S2，並且 laterally include nerve root extensions。

Fields 通常是 two lateral beams for whole brain，加上一個或多個 PA spine fields。Multiple spine fields 要把 match point 放在 spinal cord anterior，以避免 cord hotspot，並且定期 feather junction，常見是每 5 fractions 或每 9 Gy 移動 0.5–1 cm。

六、Protons vs photons for CSI

CSI 是 pediatric RT 中最典型「proton 有明確 dosimetric 與 clinical rationale」的場景。Kahalley et al. 比較同一類 medulloblastoma protocols 下 proton vs photon CSI，photon group 有 global IQ、working memory、processing speed 顯著下降；proton group 除 processing speed 外，其餘 domain 較穩定。

Peds CNS high-yield：Posterior fossa tumor 不要隨便 biopsy；多數情況 upfront maximal safe resection。Postop brain MRI 48–72 hr；spine MRI/LP 約 10–14 days postop。CSI 要 cover cribriform plate 與 entire thecal sac 到 S1–S2，junction 要 feather。Proton CSI 可減少 neurocognitive decline。

Part I. Shared principles in pediatric radiation oncology

1. Core logic of pediatric cancer

The main difference between pediatric cancer and adult oncology is that cure rates are often high, but the cost of late toxicity is also high.

The goal of pediatric RT is not simply to deliver enough dose. The goal is multimodality, risk-adapted treatment; precise balancing of cure and long-term toxicity; use of the smallest necessary volume and dose; and constant attention to growth, neurocognition, endocrine function, fertility, and second malignancy. When surgery or chemotherapy can achieve control, RT should not be overused; however, disease control should not be sacrificed for de-escalation.

This principle appears throughout pediatric oncology: medulloblastoma trials attempted CSI dose reduction, but 18 Gy CSI in average-risk children age 3–7 worsened EFS/OS; ependymoma emphasizes extent of resection as the most important prognostic factor, but RT should not automatically be delayed solely because age is under 3; neuroblastoma data show that 21.6 Gy is standard and gross-residual boost does not improve outcome; Wilms tumor requires RT to start by day 10–14 because timing affects local control.

Part II. Pediatric CNS overview and CSI

1. Pediatric CNS tumor epidemiology

Primary CNS tumors account for about 15–20% of pediatric malignancies. They are the second most common pediatric malignancy after hematologic cancers, the most common pediatric solid organ tumor, and the leading cause of cancer death in children age 0–14. Pediatric CNS tumors are more often infratentorial, about 60–70%, and more often low-grade.

2. Presentation by location

3. General workup

Unstable vital signs, altered mental status, or suspicion for increased intracranial pressure—such as headache, nausea/vomiting, or papilledema—requires emergency evaluation. Workup includes a full neurologic examination, skin examination for tumor-predisposition syndromes, and fundoscopy for papilledema. In emergencies, non-contrast head CT can assess hydrocephalus, impending herniation, or hemorrhage; brain MRI with and without contrast is the gold standard.

Postoperative brain MRI should be obtained within 48–72 hours to avoid interpretation problems from maturing blood products and inflammation. Spine MRI can be obtained preoperatively or 10–14 days postoperatively. Lumbar puncture and CSF cytology are usually performed 10–14 days after surgery to reduce herniation risk and false-positive results.

4. General management by tumor type

Most pediatric CNS tumors require upfront maximal safe resection. Germ cell tumors are a key exception and are usually managed with biopsy only.

5. CSI technique

CSI simulation is usually supine with shoulders down and neck extended, minimizing spinal curvature. A 5-point mask immobilizes the head and shoulders; the body is supported with a large vac-loc, and a knee sponge can reduce lordosis. Contours include the whole brain, covering the cribriform plate and crista galli, and the entire thecal sac to approximately S1–S2 with lateral nerve-root extensions.

Fields commonly include two lateral whole-brain beams plus one or more posterior-anterior spine fields. For multiple spine fields, the match point should be placed anterior to the spinal cord to avoid a cord hotspot, and junctions should be feathered regularly, commonly every 5 fractions or every 9 Gy by 0.5–1 cm.

6. Protons versus photons for CSI

CSI is one of the clearest pediatric settings in which protons have strong dosimetric and clinical rationale. In comparable medulloblastoma protocols, photon CSI was associated with significant declines in global IQ, working memory, and processing speed, whereas proton CSI showed greater stability except for processing speed.

Pediatric CNS high-yield: Do not casually biopsy posterior fossa tumors; most require upfront maximal safe resection. Obtain postoperative brain MRI at 48–72 hours and spine MRI/LP about 10–14 days postoperatively. CSI must cover the cribriform plate and entire thecal sac to S1–S2, with feathered junctions. Proton CSI can reduce neurocognitive decline.

髓母細胞瘤

Medulloblastoma：背景、分期、風險分層、RT 劑量與證據

第三部分：Medulloblastoma

一、Background

Medulloblastoma 是 childhood 最常見 malignant brain tumor，約佔 childhood brain cancers 20%、posterior fossa tumors 40%、所有 CNS primary tumors 5–10%。Peak incidence 在 5–9 歲。常見 genetic syndromes 包括 Gorlin/NBCCS with PTCH1、Turcot type 2/FAP with APC、Li-Fraumeni with TP53、NF1。

Medulloblastoma 是 small round blue cell tumor，以前常被歸為 PNET；classic histology 是 Homer-Wright rosettes。它通常源自 developing cerebellum 的 external germinal layer granule cell precursors，常位於 midline posterior fossa / vermis，會壓迫 fourth ventricle 造成 hydrocephalus 和 increased ICP，並可沿 subarachnoid system dissemination，形成 spinal “drop mets”。

二、Presentation and workup

典型症狀是 increased ICP：headache、morning emesis、papilledema、diplopia/CN VI palsy。Infants 可表現為 bulging anterior fontanelle 或 cranial suture splitting。Cerebellar signs 包括 truncal ataxia、dysmetria、dysdiadochokinesia、spasticity；也可有 Parinaud syndrome、upward gaze palsy、setting sun sign。

若 imaging 懷疑 posterior fossa mass，原則是 upfront maximal safe resection，不是 biopsy。Preop imaging 包括 CT head without contrast、MRI brain with/without contrast、MRI spine if feasible。Postop MRI brain 要在 72 hours 內完成；MRI spine 若 preop 未做，建議 postop 14–21 days 後做。LP/CSF cytology 若 preop ICP 高通常不做，postop 至少 >10 days，通常 14–21 days，避免 false positive。

因為 20–25% 病人 presentation 時已有 leptomeningeal disease，而且 CSF alone 和 MRI spine alone 都可能漏診，所以 MRI spine + CSF cytology 都重要。

三、Staging and risk stratification

Part III. Medulloblastoma

1. Background

Medulloblastoma is the most common malignant childhood brain tumor. It accounts for about 20% of childhood brain cancers, 40% of posterior fossa tumors, and 5–10% of all primary CNS tumors. Peak incidence is age 5–9. Associated syndromes include Gorlin/NBCCS with PTCH1, Turcot type 2/FAP with APC, Li-Fraumeni with TP53, and NF1.

Medulloblastoma is a small round blue cell tumor formerly grouped with PNET. Classic histology includes Homer-Wright rosettes. It often arises from developing cerebellar external germinal layer granule-cell precursors, is commonly midline in the posterior fossa/vermis, compresses the fourth ventricle causing hydrocephalus and increased intracranial pressure, and can disseminate through the subarachnoid system as spinal drop metastases.

2. Presentation and workup

Typical symptoms reflect increased intracranial pressure: headache, morning emesis, papilledema, and diplopia/CN VI palsy. Infants may have a bulging anterior fontanelle or cranial suture splitting. Cerebellar findings include truncal ataxia, dysmetria, dysdiadochokinesia, and spasticity; Parinaud syndrome, upward gaze palsy, and setting-sun sign may occur.

If imaging suggests a posterior fossa mass, the principle is upfront maximal safe resection, not biopsy. Preoperative imaging includes non-contrast head CT, brain MRI with and without contrast, and spine MRI if feasible. Postoperative brain MRI should be completed within 72 hours. If spine MRI was not performed preoperatively, obtain it about 14–21 days postoperatively. LP/CSF cytology is usually delayed until more than 10 days postoperatively, commonly 14–21 days, especially if preoperative intracranial pressure was high.

Because 20–25% of patients have leptomeningeal disease at presentation, and CSF cytology alone or spine MRI alone can miss disease, both spine MRI and CSF cytology are important.

3. Staging and risk stratification

In modified Chang staging, T stage now has limited prognostic value; M stage is most important.

M2Intracranial tumor beyond the primary site

Classic risk stratification uses age, metastatic status, extent of resection, histology, and MYC/MYCN features. Average-risk 5-year OS exceeds 80%; high-risk OS is about 60%. Adverse factors include age under 3, metastatic disease, subtotal resection with residual >1.5 cm², brainstem involvement, Group 3/4 molecular profile, MYC/MYCN amplification, and anaplastic/large-cell morphology.

4. Treatment paradigm

  1. Maximal safe resection, aiming for gross or near-total resection with residual <1.5 cm².
  2. Complete staging with postoperative brain MRI at 48–72 hours, spine MRI, and LP.
  3. For age >3 years, CSI plus boost.
  4. Concurrent weekly vincristine with or without carboplatin.
  5. Adjuvant cisplatin-based maintenance chemotherapy.

RT usually starts about 1 month after surgery. The RT course should ideally be completed within 45 days; longer duration can impair outcome.

室管膜瘤

Ependymoma：局部控制疾病、手術切除程度、focal RT 與 CSI 角色

第四部分：Ependymoma

一、Background

Ependymoma 是 uncommon tumor，約佔 pediatric CNS tumors 6%。Intracranial ependymoma peak incidence 在 early childhood，median diagnosis age 3–5 years，slight male predominance。Spinal ependymoma 在 children rare、adults more common，並與 NF2-related schwannomas 相關。Ependymoma 起源於 glial stem cells / ependymal cells lining the ventricles。

Children 的 ependymoma 最常在 posterior fossa，通常與 fourth ventricle 接觸，常延伸到 subarachnoid space；約 2/3 posterior fossa，1/3 supratentorial。成人則多為 intramedullary spinal cord，常在 cervical/thoracic spine。Ependymoma presentation 時通常不太會 metastatic。

二、Presentation and workup

Children 常因 increased ICP 表現：headache、nausea、ataxia、vertigo、papilledema。Workup 包括 H&P focusing on elevated ICP、MRI brain and spine with/without contrast、LP for CSF cytology；若 ICP elevated，LP 應等 postop 10–14 days 以避免 herniation。

治療第一步是 maximal safe resection，之後 postop MRI 評估 extent of resection。Elevated ICP 可用 ETV 或 VP shunt 處理。ETV 是在 third ventricle floor 做 fenestration，讓 CSF drain to prepontine subarachnoid space；VP shunt 是把 CSF drain 到 peritoneum。一般 ETV infection/complication 較少，但 very young children 可能 VP shunt failure rate 較低。

三、Classification

2021 WHO classification 依三個因素：anatomic site、WHO grade、molecular features。Anatomic site 包括 supratentorial、posterior fossa、spinal。WHO grade：grade 1 subependymoma；grade 2 classic/differentiated and myxopapillary ependymoma；grade 3 anaplastic。

四、Treatment paradigm

  1. Maximal safe resection，aim for GTR。
  2. Complete staging：MRI brain postop within 72 hr、MRI spine、LP postop 10–14 days。
  3. Local RT to tumor bed，通常 59.4 Gy；若 <18 months and GTR 可 54 Gy。
  4. STR 時可短程 CHT 嘗試 convert to resectable disease，再 second surgery。
  5. CSI only for metastatic disease / positive CSF / positive MRI neuroaxis, and age ≥3 years。

Part IV. Ependymoma

1. Background

Ependymoma is uncommon and accounts for about 6% of pediatric CNS tumors. Intracranial ependymoma peaks in early childhood, with median diagnosis age 3–5 years and slight male predominance. Spinal ependymoma is rare in children, more common in adults, and associated with NF2-related schwannomas. It arises from glial stem cells or ependymal cells lining the ventricles.

In children, ependymoma is most often posterior fossa, commonly contacting the fourth ventricle and extending into the subarachnoid space; about two-thirds are posterior fossa and one-third supratentorial. In adults, ependymoma is more often intramedullary spinal cord, usually cervical or thoracic. Metastatic disease at presentation is uncommon.

2. Presentation and workup

Children often present with increased intracranial pressure: headache, nausea, ataxia, vertigo, and papilledema. Workup includes history and physical examination focused on increased ICP, MRI brain and spine with and without contrast, and LP for CSF cytology. If ICP is elevated, LP should wait until 10–14 days postoperatively to avoid herniation.

The first treatment step is maximal safe resection, followed by postoperative MRI to assess extent of resection. Elevated ICP can be treated with endoscopic third ventriculostomy or VP shunt. ETV fenestrates the third ventricle floor to drain CSF to the prepontine subarachnoid space; VP shunt drains CSF to the peritoneum. ETV generally has fewer infections and complications, but very young children may have lower VP shunt failure rates.

3. Classification

The 2021 WHO classification uses anatomic site, WHO grade, and molecular features. Sites include supratentorial, posterior fossa, and spinal. Grades include grade 1 subependymoma, grade 2 classic/differentiated and myxopapillary ependymoma, and grade 3 anaplastic ependymoma.

4. Treatment paradigm

  1. Maximal safe resection aiming for gross total resection.
  2. Complete staging with postoperative brain MRI within 72 hours, spine MRI, and LP 10–14 days postoperatively.
  3. Local RT to the tumor bed, usually 59.4 Gy; 54 Gy can be used for age <18 months after GTR.
  4. After subtotal resection, short-course chemotherapy may attempt to convert to resectable disease, followed by second surgery.
  5. CSI only for metastatic disease, positive CSF, or positive neuroaxis MRI, and age ≥3 years.

Part VIII. Ewing sarcoma

1. Background

Ewing sarcoma is the second most common bone tumor in children, with male predominance around 1.5:1 and peak age about 15 years. It is radiosensitive, unlike osteosarcoma. Ewing sarcoma is defined by EWSR1 translocation: t(11;22) is most common, about 90%; t(21;22) accounts for about 5–10%. Nonmetastatic 5-year OS is about 70–80%; metastatic OS is about 30%.

Ewing sarcoma is a small round blue cell tumor. The mnemonic MEN SLURP includes medulloblastoma, Ewing, neuroblastoma, small cell carcinoma, lymphoma, undifferentiated sarcoma, rhabdomyosarcoma, and PNET.

2. Anatomy and presentation

About half of Ewing tumors are central—pelvis 45%, chest wall 35%, spine 10%—and half are extremity tumors, often diaphyseal. The mnemonic EG-MODE: epiphysis = giant cell tumor; metaphysis = osteosarcoma; diaphysis = Ewing.

Presentation includes pain in >90%, swelling/mass in 65%, limited movement in 25%, neurologic changes in 15% and up to 50% for central tumors, pathologic fracture in 15%, and fever in 10%. About 20–25% present with metastases, commonly lung and bone.
